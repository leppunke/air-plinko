# Blower Duel Plinko — Build Plan

## Concept Summary
Open-mesh plinko board standing vertically between two players. Players wield M18 blowers to steer balls through air ports OR blast their opponent in the face through the permeable mesh. The board is see-through — players face each other across 3 feet of pegs, balls, and wind.

---

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Width | 48" (4 ft) | Full playing field width |
| Height | 60" (5 ft) | Playable standing for kids & adults |
| Depth (gap) | 4" | Whiffle ball (2.75") + clearance. Tighter than original — keeps balls in peg contact |
| Player spacing | ~36" (3 ft) | Board depth (4") + clearance each side. Close enough for blower to reach opponent |
| Tilt angle | Vertical (90°) | No tilt — balls drop straight, air is the only steering force |

Total footprint with frame: ~48"W × 48"D × 72"H (including top beacon mount)

## Architecture

### 1. Frame — The Arena

**Vertical standing frame** — no tilt. The board stands straight up like an arcade cabinet.

- **Main frame:** 2×4 lumber, rectangular box: 48"W × 60"H
- **Base feet:** Wide 2×4 L-brackets on each side (24" deep) for freestanding stability — like a sandwich board
- **Sandbag hooks:** Eye bolts at each base foot for sandbag anchoring on windy days
- **Top crossbar:** Extends 6" above the playing field — mount point for the M18 tower light beacon
- **Side uprights:** Extend 6" beyond the playing field on each side — mount points for bungee tether anchors

### 2. Playing Field — The Open Arena

The key difference from standard plinko: **both faces are permeable to air**.

**Back panel (Player 1 side):**
- ½" hardware cloth (galvanized welded wire mesh, ½" × ½" grid) stretched across the full 48" × 60" frame opening
- Stapled to the inside face of the 2×4 frame with heavy-duty staples every 3"
- Fine mesh netting (¼" batting cage netting) layered over the hardware cloth on the inside — catches balls, passes air freely

**Front panel (Player 2 side):**
- Identical construction: ½" hardware cloth + fine mesh netting
- Mirrored mounting on the opposite inside face of the frame

**Gap between panels:** 4" — maintained by the 2×4 frame depth (actual 3.5") plus ½" spacer strips on each side

**Peg field:**
- ¾" wooden dowels, 4" long (spanning the full gap between mesh panels)
- Drilled through both hardware cloth layers and seated in small mounting blocks screwed to the frame's inner perimeter strips
- Grid pattern: 6" horizontal spacing, 5" vertical spacing, offset alternating rows (classic plinko)
- ~56 pegs total (8 columns × 7 rows, offset)
- Each peg passes through both mesh layers — structurally locks the two mesh faces together

**Why this works:**
- Whiffle balls (2.75") cannot fit through ½" mesh — fully contained
- Air passes freely through mesh in both directions
- Players can SEE each other through the mesh and pegs
- Pegs serve double duty: plinko deflectors + structural spacers holding mesh taut

### 3. Air Ports (×4 — 2 per side)

Each player gets two dedicated air ports — openings in the frame where the blower nozzle seats.

**Port positions:**
- **LOW port:** 18" from bottom, on the side rail — aims air across the lower peg field
- **HIGH port:** 42" from bottom, on the side rail — aims air across the upper peg field

**Port construction:**
- 3" diameter hole through the 2×4 side rail
- Short PVC pipe sleeve (3" ID × 4" long) press-fit into the hole — smooth surface for nozzle insertion
- Rubber gasket ring on the outside face — seats the M18 blower nozzle snugly
- Flared funnel on the inside face — spreads the air blast from a focused jet to a ~30° cone across the field
- The port aims slightly upward (~10°) so air pushes balls up and across rather than straight through

**The open zone above the board:**
- No port needed — the top of the board is open above the 60" frame
- Players can aim the blower OVER the top of the board directly at their opponent's face/upper body
- This is the "face blast" zone — no structural modification needed, just tether length that allows aiming up and over

### 4. Scoring System

**Bottom trough** — full-width angled collection at the bottom of the playing field, divided into scoring zones:

```
|  GUTTER | 10 | 25 | 50 | JACKPOT | 50 | 25 | 10 | GUTTER |
|   0pts  |    |    |    |  100pts |    |    |    |   0pts  |
```

**Construction:**
- 7 vertical dividers (¼" plywood fins, 4" tall) mounted inside the bottom of the frame
- Divider spacing (from left): 4" | 6" | 5" | 4" | 3" | 4" | 5" | 6" | 4"
- Each zone is a pocket — ball drops in and stays
- Bottom of each pocket is a hinged trap door on a shared rod — operator pulls a lever to release all balls at once for the next round
- Score labels: Large vinyl numbers on the front edge of each divider, visible from both sides

### 5. Ball Drop Slots

**Top of the board** — 3 drop positions along the top rail:

- **Left slot:** 12" from left edge
- **Center slot:** Dead center (24" from each edge)
- **Right slot:** 12" from right edge

**Construction:**
- 3" wide × 1" deep notches cut into the top rail
- Small angled ramp (2" long) on each side of the notch so the ball funnels into the peg field
- Players reach up and drop the ball in from their side — mesh is only on the faces, top is open
- A horizontal bar across each slot prevents reaching into the playing field — just wide enough for a ball

### 6. Blower Tether System

**Critical for safety and gameplay balance.**

- **Tether:** 4ft bungee cord (not rigid — allows natural aiming motion)
- **Anchor point:** Eye bolt on the side upright, at player's shoulder height (~48" from ground for kids, adjustable)
- **Blower attachment:** Carabiner clip to the M18 blower's rear handle mount point
- **Range of motion with tether:**
  - CAN reach both air ports on their side
  - CAN aim up and over the board at opponent's face
  - CAN aim through the mesh at opponent's ball zone
  - CANNOT aim below opponent's waist (tether geometry prevents it)
  - CANNOT walk away with the blower

### 7. Frame Stability & Portability

**Stability:**
- Base feet extend 24" on each side (4 L-brackets total) — wide stance
- Sandbag hooks on all 4 feet — 25lb sandbags for outdoor use
- Total weight with hardware: ~80 lbs — heavy enough to not wobble during play

**Portability / Breakdown:**
- Frame assembled with carriage bolts + wing nuts at all joints
- Mesh panels stapled to removable inner frame strips (unscrew strips, mesh rolls up)
- Base feet fold flat against the main frame (hinged)
- Breaks down to: 1 flat frame (48"×60"×4"), 4 folded feet, 2 rolled mesh panels, bag of pegs
- Two people can carry the assembled unit with side-mounted handles
- Label all joints (A1, A2, etc.) for foolproof reassembly

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Frame (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut 2×4s for main rectangular frame: 2 × 60" uprights, 2 × 48" crossbars
2. Assemble with carriage bolts + wing nuts (no glue — must break down)
3. Cut and attach 4 base L-bracket feet with hinges
4. Install side-mount carry handles
5. Install eye bolts for tether anchors and sandbag hooks
6. Cut top crossbar extension (6" above frame) for beacon mount
7. Test stability — should stand freely without sandbags on flat ground

### Phase 2: Mesh & Pegs (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Cut hardware cloth to 48" × 60" (2 panels)
2. Cut batting cage netting to match (2 panels)
3. Create removable inner frame strips (1×2 lumber) for mesh mounting
4. Staple mesh + netting to inner strips
5. Mount inner strips to main frame with screws
6. Mark peg positions on both mesh panels (6" H × 5" V, offset grid)
7. Drill peg holes through both mesh layers at each position
8. Insert 4" dowel pegs through both layers — press fit, dab of wood glue on mounting blocks
9. Test: shake the frame — pegs should lock both mesh faces together rigidly

### Phase 3: Air Ports & Scoring (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Drill 3" holes in side rails for air ports (2 per side, 4 total)
2. Insert PVC sleeve pipes, secure with construction adhesive
3. Attach rubber gaskets on exterior face
4. Attach funnel/bell-end diffusers on interior face
5. Build scoring trough: bottom rail + 7 plywood dividers
6. Install hinged trap door mechanism for ball release
7. Cut top drop slot notches (3 positions) with ball-only guard bars
8. Apply vinyl scoring labels

### Phase 4: Finish & Test (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Paint frame black (or Milwaukee red/black scheme)
2. Paint scoring zones in bright yellow (JACKPOT), white (HIGH), gray (MID/LOW)
3. Attach bungee tethers to eye bolts, carabiners to blowers
4. Mount M18 tower light on top crossbar
5. Load balls, fire up blowers, PLAY TEST
6. Tune: adjust funnel angles on air ports for best ball deflection
7. Test face-blast range: verify blower at full tether reaches opponent's face but not below waist
8. Test mesh containment: blast at full power, verify no balls escape
9. Final sandbag stability test with two players actively playing
