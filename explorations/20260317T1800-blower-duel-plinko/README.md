# BLOWER DUEL PLINKO

## The Pitch

Two players. Two M18 blowers. One open-air plinko board between them. You can aim your blower at the board to score — or aim it at your opponent's FACE to sabotage their turn. Every blast is a choice: **help yourself or wreck your enemy**.

The kid across from you just dropped a ball headed for the jackpot? Forget the board. Aim that 120 MPH wind cannon straight at their face. Hair flying. Eyes squinting. Laughing so hard they can't even pull their own trigger. Their ball drifts into the gutter. You win.

This is the game your kid tells their friends about on Monday.

## Concept

A 4ft × 5ft plinko board stands vertically between two players who face each other from opposite sides. The board has an **open mesh face** on each side — not sealed acrylic. Think hardware cloth or chicken wire backed by clear netting. Balls stay in. Air passes through.

Each player wields an M18 blower on a bungee tether. The blower nozzle can:
1. **Aim at the board** through side air ports → steer your ball toward scoring zones
2. **Aim OVER the board** at your opponent → blast them in the face, disrupting their aim and concentration
3. **Aim THROUGH the mesh** at their side of the board → push THEIR ball into the gutter

The board is narrow enough (3ft between players) that a full-power blower blast reaches the other side. The mesh lets air through but keeps balls contained. Players are close enough to see each other's faces — and close enough for the blower to hit.

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Each player gets 5 colored whiffle balls (Red vs Black)
- Players stand facing each other across the board, ~18" from their side
- Each side has 2 air ports (LOW and HIGH) plus open space above the board for face shots

### Turn Flow — SIMULTANEOUS PLAY
Unlike taking turns, both players drop balls and blast at the same time. Pure real-time chaos.

1. **DROP** — Either player can drop a ball into any of 3 top slots whenever they're ready
2. **DECIDE** — Ball is falling through pegs. Do you:
   - Blast your air port to steer YOUR ball toward a scoring zone?
   - Blast OVER the board at your opponent's face so they can't aim?
   - Blast THROUGH the mesh at THEIR ball to push it into the gutter?
3. **CHAOS** — Both players blasting creates crosswinds. Balls go sideways. Hair goes sideways. Everything goes sideways.
4. **SCORE** — Balls land in scoring pockets at the bottom

### The Sabotage Mechanic
This is what makes it. The blower has THREE uses:

| Target | Effect | Risk |
|--------|--------|------|
| **Your air port** → your ball | Steer toward high scores | You're not defending |
| **Over the board** → opponent's face | They flinch, laugh, can't aim | Your ball is unguided |
| **Through the mesh** → opponent's ball | Push their ball to gutter | Your ball is unguided |

The strategic tension: **every second you spend on offense is a second your own ball is falling unguided.** Do you play your game or ruin theirs?

### Scoring Pockets (Bottom of Board)
| Zone | Points | Width |
|------|--------|-------|
| JACKPOT (dead center) | 100 | 3" |
| HIGH (inner) | 50 | 4" × 2 |
| MID (middle) | 25 | 5" × 2 |

### Winning
- All 5 balls each, highest score wins
- **DUEL BONUS**: If you blast your opponent's ball into the gutter while scoring your own ball in the same drop, +50 bonus

## Board Design — Open Mesh

The key innovation: **the board is see-through and air-permeable**.

- **Back structure**: Standard plywood with peg grid
- **Front face**: Hardware cloth (½" galvanized mesh) stretched tight across the frame
- **Inner netting**: Fine mesh netting (like batting cage material) behind the hardware cloth — catches balls, passes air
- **Why it works**: Whiffle balls are 2.75" and can't fit through ½" mesh. But air flows freely through. Players can see each other through the board. Air from one side affects the other.

This means the board isn't a wall — it's an arena. You're looking at your opponent through the pegs, watching their ball fall, deciding whether to attack or defend.

## What Makes This Hit Different

### The Choice
Every other carnival game is single-player optimization. Hit the target. Knock down the bottles. This game has a **social decision** at its core: be good, or be bad? Help yourself, or hurt your friend? That choice — and the immediate, visceral feedback of a blower to the face — is what makes kids play 10 times in a row.

### The Physical Comedy
A kid getting blasted in the face with a leaf blower is objectively hilarious. It's not painful. It's not scary. It's funny. The face they make. The hair going crazy. The full-body flinch. Every single time, it gets a laugh from the crowd.

### The Drama
"HE'S GOING FOR THE FACE! WATCH OUT!" The crowd naturally narrates. There are heroes and villains. Comebacks. Betrayals. A kid who was aiming for the board suddenly pivots to blast their opponent at the last second. It's a story every time.

### The Replayability
You can play it cooperative (both aim at the board, combine scores). You can play it cutthroat (ignore your own score, just blast your opponent every round). You can play it strategic (blast their ball, then quick-aim your own). Every pairing plays it differently.

## Safety

- Blowers on bungee tethers — limited range of motion, can't be aimed below the waist
- Air only — no projectiles, no impact. Just wind. Completely harmless at 3ft distance
- Mesh and netting keep all balls contained
- Tether length calibrated: blower reaches the board and opponent's face area, but not their body below shoulders
- Eye protection optional but available (safety glasses at the booth — lean into the construction theme)
- Operator can pull batteries to kill power instantly
