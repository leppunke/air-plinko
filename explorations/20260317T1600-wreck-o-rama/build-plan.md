# WRECK-O-RAMA — Build Plan

## Concept
A wrecking ball demolition carnival game. Players swing a tethered padded wrecking ball at a tower of wooden blocks stacked on a rotating turntable. Score based on blocks knocked off the platform. Different colored blocks have different point values. One swing per turn.

## Architecture

### 1. Gantry Frame (The Overhead Structure)

The gantry is an upside-down U-shape — two vertical posts with a horizontal beam across the top. The wrecking ball hangs from the center of the horizontal beam.

- **Uprights (×2):** 4×4 pressure-treated posts, 8 ft tall
- **Horizontal beam:** 4×4 post, 5 ft long, bolted across the top of the uprights with heavy-duty corner brackets
- **Diagonal braces (×4):** 2×4 lumber, 3 ft long, at 45° from each upright to the horizontal beam (two per side, front and back) — prevents racking
- **Base feet (×2):** T-shaped base made from 2×4 lumber. Each upright sits on a 4-ft wide cross piece, creating a stable floor footprint. Cross pieces run perpendicular to the horizontal beam
- **Anchor options:** Sandbags on base feet (indoor), or stake into ground (outdoor)
- **Assembly:** Carriage bolts + wing nuts at all joints. Gantry breaks down into 5 pieces (2 uprights, 1 beam, 2 base feet) for transport

### 2. Wrecking Ball

- **Core:** 8" diameter foam ball (Nerf-style, available at sporting goods stores) OR a lightweight rubber ball wrapped in duct tape for weight
- **Weight target:** 3-4 lbs — heavy enough to scatter blocks, light enough for a 6-year-old to swing
- **Shell:** Canvas drawstring bag around the foam core, cinched tight. The bag has a reinforced grommet at the top
- **Rope:** ½" braided nylon rope, 6 ft long, tied through the bag's grommet at one end and through an eye bolt at the other
- **Overhead mount:** ½" eye bolt through the center of the horizontal beam, with a heavy washer and lock nut on top. Rope ties to the eye bolt with a bowline knot
- **Safety tether:** A second lighter cord (paracord) from the ball to the eye bolt as a backup in case the main rope fails. Slightly longer than the main rope so it only engages if needed

### 3. Turntable

- **Base disc:** 24" diameter circle cut from ¾" plywood
- **Bearing:** 12" heavy-duty lazy susan bearing (rated 300+ lbs), bolted to the center of the base disc and to a lower platform disc
- **Platform disc:** 24" diameter circle cut from ¾" plywood, sits under the base disc. This is the fixed piece that sits on the ground/table
- **Rim:** ½" tall lip around the edge of the top disc (thin plywood strip bent and glued/stapled around the perimeter). Keeps blocks from sliding off during rotation but allows them to fly off on impact
- **Mounting:** The bottom platform disc screws into a 30"×30" piece of ¾" plywood (the "stage") which provides a flat, stable surface and defines the scoring boundary. Blocks that leave this stage = scored
- **Spin mechanism:** Operator gives the turntable a push by hand. The lazy susan bearing provides low-friction rotation. Expect 3-5 RPM, decaying over ~30 seconds. Enough for 2-3 full rotations per turn

### 4. Tower Blocks

- **Dimensions:** Each block is 6" × 3" × 1.5" (similar to a large Jenga block)
- **Material:** Pine or poplar — lightweight, cheap, takes paint well
- **Quantity:** 50 blocks total (30 standard + 8 silver + 4 gold + 4 skull + 1 crown + 3 spare)
- **Cutting:** Rip 1×4 lumber (actual 3.5" wide) to 3" width, then crosscut to 6" lengths. A single 8-ft 1×4 yields ~15 blocks. Need 4 boards
- **Paint scheme:**
  - Standard: Clear coat or light stain (natural wood)
  - Silver: Metallic silver spray paint
  - Gold: Metallic gold spray paint
  - Skull: Flat black with white skull stencil (stencil from 3D-printed template)
  - Crown: Red with gold crown stencil, sits in a 3D-printed crown-shaped holder on top
- **Stacking pattern:** 3 blocks per layer laid side-by-side (like Jenga), alternating 90° each layer. 8-10 layers = 24-30 blocks per tower. Remaining blocks are spares/rotation

### 5. Scoring & Collection

- **Scoring boundary:** The 30"×30" stage platform. Any block that leaves this platform = scored at its face value
- **Crown Zone:** A 12"×12" target square painted on the ground, 3 ft in front of the turntable (toward the player). The Crown block only scores its 500 pts if it lands in or touching this zone
- **Collection walls:** Three 2-ft tall walls made from ¼" plywood or foam board, forming a U-shape around the back and sides of the turntable. These catch flying blocks and keep them in the play area. Open on the player-facing side so blocks can fly toward the player (and toward the Crown Zone)
- **Block bin:** A crate or box next to the operator for collecting scored blocks between turns

### 6. Safety Features

- **Swing limiter:** The 6-ft rope naturally limits the wrecking ball's reach. The gantry is positioned so the ball can reach the tower but cannot swing past it into the operator area
- **Player line:** A painted/taped line on the ground 4 ft from the turntable center. Player must stand behind this line (the rope length allows them to swing from here)
- **Side barriers:** The collection walls also prevent blocks from flying into the spectator area on the sides. The open front faces only the player
- **Foam padding:** Wrecking ball is foam-padded. Even a mis-swing hitting a bystander is a bonk, not an injury
- **Weight limit:** 4 lbs max on the ball keeps impact forces kid-safe

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Blocks & Turntable (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Rip 1×4 boards to 3" width on table saw
2. Crosscut to 6" lengths — yield ~50 blocks
3. Sand all blocks (round edges slightly — splinter prevention)
4. Sort into groups: 30 standard, 8 silver, 4 gold, 4 skull, 1 crown, 3 spare
5. Paint/stain each group. Clear coat all blocks after paint dries
6. Cut two 24" plywood circles (jigsaw or router with circle jig)
7. Bolt lazy susan bearing between the two discs
8. Cut 30"×30" plywood stage, mount bottom turntable disc to center
9. Add ½" rim strip around top disc edge

### Phase 2: Gantry Frame (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Cut 4×4 posts: two at 8 ft (uprights), one at 5 ft (beam)
2. Cut 2×4 diagonal braces: four at ~3 ft, with 45° miters
3. Cut 2×4 base feet: two at 4 ft
4. Drill bolt holes at all joints
5. Dry-assemble gantry with carriage bolts + wing nuts
6. Install corner brackets at beam-to-upright connections
7. Attach diagonal braces
8. Drill center of horizontal beam, install eye bolt
9. Test stability — gantry should not wobble when beam is pulled laterally

### Phase 3: Wrecking Ball & Rigging (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Insert foam ball into canvas bag, cinch tight
2. If more weight needed, add a small sandbag or fishing weights inside the bag (target 3-4 lbs)
3. Tie ½" nylon rope to bag grommet (bowline knot)
4. Tie other end to eye bolt (bowline knot)
5. Attach paracord safety tether — same connection points, 6" slack
6. Test swing: ball should reach 4 ft forward of the gantry center at full extension
7. Adjust rope length if needed

### Phase 4: Collection Walls & Finishing (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Cut three 2 ft × 3 ft panels from ¼" plywood or foam board
2. Hinge or bracket panels together in a U-shape
3. Position U-shape around back and sides of turntable stage
4. Mark player line (floor tape) 4 ft from turntable center
5. Mark Crown Zone (floor tape) — 12"×12" square, 3 ft from turntable toward player
6. Create stencils for skull and crown designs (3D print or cut from cardboard)
7. Apply stencils to skull and crown blocks
8. Stack a test tower: 3 blocks per layer, alternating 90°, 8-10 layers
9. Test swing — full demolition run. Verify:
   - Ball reaches tower at full swing
   - Blocks scatter satisfyingly (not too heavy)
   - Collection walls catch most flying blocks
   - Turntable rotates smoothly when pushed
   - Gantry is stable during impact vibration
10. Adjust and enjoy

## Operator Guide (Game Day)

### Setup (~5 min)
1. Assemble gantry (wing nut joints)
2. Place sandbags on base feet
3. Set turntable stage on ground in front of gantry
4. Position collection walls
5. Mark player line and crown zone with tape
6. Hang wrecking ball from eye bolt
7. Stack first tower

### Running the Game (~30 sec per player)
1. Stack tower on turntable (3×layers, alternating, 8-10 high)
2. Place gold blocks in lower-middle layers, skulls in outer positions
3. Place crown on top in its holder
4. Give turntable a spin
5. Hand player the wrecking ball, instruct them to stand behind the line
6. "3... 2... 1... WRECK IT!"
7. Player swings
8. Count scored blocks (off the platform), calculate points
9. Announce score, collect blocks into bin
10. Re-stack for next player (~90 seconds)

### Tips
- Pre-stack 2-3 towers worth of block arrangements on a side table to speed up resets
- Recruit a second volunteer to count/collect blocks while you stack
- For little kids: shorter tower (4-5 layers), no spin, shorter rope
- For big kids: taller tower, faster spin, add extra skull blocks
- Keep spare blocks handy — some will get scuffed or chipped over the day

## Transport & Storage
- Gantry breaks down into 5 pieces (2 uprights, 1 beam, 2 base feet) — fits in a truck bed or SUV with seats down
- Turntable is one piece, 30"×30" — slides flat against anything
- Collection walls fold flat
- Blocks go in a milk crate or 5-gallon bucket
- Total packed volume: roughly 8 ft × 2 ft × 1 ft
- Total weight: ~80-100 lbs (gantry is the heavy part)
