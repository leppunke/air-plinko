# WRECK-O-RAMA: The Wrecking Ball Game

## Concept

Every kid wants to swing a wrecking ball. Now they can.

A 5-foot tower of colorful wooden blocks sits on a slowly rotating turntable. The player grabs a padded wrecking ball (bowling-ball sized, tethered to an overhead beam by a 6-foot rope) and gets **ONE SWING** to demolish the tower. Score is based on which blocks get knocked off the platform. Gold blocks are buried deep. Skull blocks cost you points. The turntable keeps spinning, so timing your swing actually matters.

The tower explodes. Blocks fly. Bells ring. Kids go absolutely feral.

Zero electronics. Zero rules ambiguity. Five seconds to explain. Thirty seconds to play. Two minutes to rebuild. Pure carnival chaos.

## The Pitch

"Step right up! Grab the wrecking ball! Knock down the tower! But watch out for the SKULLS!"

## Gameplay

1. **Operator stacks the tower** — 40-50 lightweight wooden blocks on a 24" rotating turntable. Gold blocks (high value) get buried in the lower-middle. Skull blocks (negative points) scattered throughout. A crown block sits on the very top.
2. **Turntable spins** — Operator gives the lazy susan a push. Tower slowly rotates (~3-5 RPM). This means the "weak side" of the tower keeps moving.
3. **Player grabs the wrecking ball** — A 4-lb foam-padded ball on a 6-foot rope, hanging from an overhead beam. Player pulls it back and lines up their swing.
4. **ONE SWING** — Player releases. The wrecking ball arcs into the tower. CRASH.
5. **Score the carnage** — Blocks that leave the turntable platform count. Blocks still on the platform don't count. Simple.

## Block Types & Scoring

| Block | Color | Points | Count | Notes |
|-------|-------|--------|-------|-------|
| Standard | Natural wood | 10 pts | 30 | Bulk of the tower |
| Silver | Silver paint | 25 pts | 8 | Mixed throughout |
| Gold | Gold paint | 100 pts | 4 | Buried in lower-middle — hard to knock out without toppling everything above |
| Skull | Black + skull stencil | -50 pts | 4 | Scattered in outer layer — easy to accidentally knock off |
| Crown | Red + gold crown | 500 pts | 1 | Sits on top — BUT only scores if it lands in the "Crown Zone" (a marked target area 3 feet away) |

**Typical score range:** 150-450 points. Perfect run (all gold, no skulls, crown lands): 1,030 points.

## What Makes It Different

- **NOT plinko.** No pegs, no gravity maze, no ball-through-grid. This is demolition.
- **Full-body involvement.** Kids are swinging, aiming, timing. It's physical.
- **Visceral satisfaction.** The CRASH of wooden blocks scattering is hardwired into the human brain as deeply satisfying.
- **Risk/reward tension.** Harder swings knock more blocks off (more points) but also knock more skulls off (negative points). A surgical precision hit might beat a full send.
- **Rotating target.** The turntable adds timing skill. You can see the "open face" of the tower rotating toward you — do you wait for it or swing now?
- **Spectator sport.** Everyone within 50 feet hears the crash and comes running.
- **Insanely fast throughput.** Swing, score, stack, next. No waiting for balls to settle, no complex mechanics.

## Difficulty Scaling

- **Kindergarteners:** Operator stacks a shorter tower (3 layers). Turntable doesn't spin. Rope is shorter. It's basically "hit the blocks."
- **2nd-3rd graders:** Full tower, slow spin. They're learning to time and aim.
- **4th-5th graders:** Full tower, faster spin. Optional: longer rope = wider arc = harder to aim. Double skull blocks for extra risk.
