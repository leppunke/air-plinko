# THE SWARM — Build Plan

## Overview
Giant honeycomb-themed plinko game with stomp pad launchers, a central Queen Bee Spinner, bee bumper mechanisms, multi-sensory elements, and candy-dispensing honey pot scoring buckets. Full Grattan bee branding.

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Board width | 48" (4 ft) | Two-player side-by-side |
| Board height | 72" (6 ft) | Tall enough for max cascade drama |
| Board depth (gap) | 5" | Whiffle ball (2.75") + airflow + Queen Spinner clearance |
| Tilt angle | ~20° from vertical | Gravity does the work |
| Peak height (on frame) | ~84" (7 ft) | Top of board visible from across the yard |
| Footprint with legs + stomp pads | ~8 ft W × 6 ft D × 7 ft H | Stomp pads extend 3ft in front of the board |

## Architecture

### 1. Frame & Playing Field

#### 1a. Backboard
- **Material:** ¾" plywood, 48×72" sheet
- **Finish:** Matte black base coat + Grattan gold honeycomb stencil pattern (hexes painted in metallic gold at ~30% opacity as background texture)
- **Peg holes:** Hex grid pattern, 4.5" center-to-center spacing, offset rows. ~90 peg positions total
- **Queen Spinner cutout:** 12" diameter circular hole at board center (~36" height), reinforced with ¾" plywood ring glued to the back
- **Bumper holes (×3):** 6" diameter holes at left-field, right-field, and above-Queen positions. Bushing-lined for smooth bumper action
- **Candy tube holes:** Seven ¾" holes behind each scoring bucket position for gravity-fed candy tubes

#### 1b. Front Panel
- **Material:** ¼" clear acrylic, 48×72"
- **Mounting:** Bolted to peg dowels that pass through both panels + edge rails
- **Queen Spinner window:** 12" diameter cutout matching backboard, with ½" acrylic ring spacer to keep balls in the playing field around the spinner

#### 1c. Edge Rails
- **Material:** 1×6 lumber (actual 5.5" depth = playing field gap when sandwiched between ¾" plywood + ¼" acrylic)
- **Configuration:** All four sides. Bottom rail has seven hexagonal cutouts for scoring buckets
- **Quantity:** ~22 linear feet total (3× 8ft boards)

#### 1d. Support Frame
- **Rear legs (×2):** 2×4 lumber, 60" long, hinged at top for fold-flat transport
- **Cross brace:** Horizontal 2×4 between legs at 24" height
- **Angle limiter:** Adjustable chain between legs and backboard — sets 20° tilt
- **Base feet:** Rubber-capped 2×4 feet + sandbag attachment points for grass/concrete stability
- **Handles (×2):** Heavy-duty rope handles on each side of the backboard for two-person carry
- **All joints:** Wing nuts + carriage bolts for tool-free assembly
- **Label all joints** (A1, A2, etc.) for foolproof year-over-year reassembly

### 2. Hex Peg Field (~90 pegs)

- **Dowels:** ¾" wooden dowels, 5" long (spanning full playing field depth)
- **Peg caps:** 3D-printed hexagonal caps (1.5" across flats) press-fit onto each dowel end at the acrylic side. Printed in Grattan gold PLA
- **Arrangement:** True hex grid (offset rows), 4.5" center-to-center. This creates 6-directional bounce chaos (not just left-right like square grids)
- **Cedar option:** Use cedar dowels for 30% of pegs (every 3rd row). Natural scent release during play
- **UV treatment:** Spray peg caps with UV-reactive clear coat. They glow under the blacklight canopy

### 3. Stomp Pad Launcher System (×2)

#### 3a. Flower Stomp Pads
- **Base:** 24" diameter circle cut from ¾" plywood
- **Shape:** Six petal cutouts from ¼" plywood, glued/screwed around the perimeter — creates a flower silhouette
- **Painting:** Bright colors — one pad red/orange, one pad purple/blue. Center hex painted gold
- **Air bladder:** Heavy-duty rubber bladder (repurposed exercise ball bladder or custom welded PVC sheet) sandwiched between the plywood top and a rigid plywood bottom plate
- **Hinge:** Piano hinge along the back edge — pad rocks up at the front when stomped, compresses bladder
- **Stomp surface:** Rubber mat on top (traction, impact absorption)
- **Mounting:** Bolted to a 2×4 base frame on the ground, 3ft in front of the main board

#### 3b. Pneumatic Launch Tubes
- **Hose:** 2" flex hose from each bladder to a launch tube at the bottom of the board
- **Launch tube:** 1.5" PVC pipe, 18" long, angled at ~70° from horizontal, mounted at the bottom-left and bottom-right of the playing field
- **Ball magazine:** Gravity-fed hopper above each launch tube. 10 balls stack vertically. After each stomp launches the bottom ball, the next drops into the chamber
- **Check valve:** Simple rubber flap at the bladder outlet prevents backflow (ball doesn't get sucked back)
- **Calibration:** Bladder volume and hose diameter tuned so that a medium stomp launches to mid-board, a monster stomp reaches the fog zone at the top

### 4. Queen Bee Spinner (center of board)

- **Drum:** 10" diameter hexagonal drum, 4.5" deep (fills the playing field gap). 3D-printed in sections from gold PLA, assembled on a central bearing
- **Bearing:** Lazy susan bearing (4" diameter) bolted to a mounting plate on the backboard
- **Entry/exit slots:** Six 3" wide slots evenly spaced around the hex drum perimeter. Balls enter from any direction, rattle inside, exit whichever slot faces a downward angle
- **Internal baffles:** Two small internal ramps (3D-printed) inside the drum that randomize ball exit direction
- **Drive:** Beekeeper's hand crank on the back of the board drives the spinner via a simple belt (timing belt or bike chain on sprockets). Crank-to-drum gear ratio ~3:1 (3 crank turns = 1 drum revolution at slow speed, fast cranking = drum whips)
- **Decoration:** Hex drum exterior painted with bee wing pattern. Crown symbol on one face. "QUEEN" in gold lettering
- **Mounting:** Accessible from the back for maintenance. Two wing-nut bolts hold the mounting plate — swap the whole drum in 2 minutes

### 5. Bee Bumper Mechanism (×3)

- **Bumper puck:** 5" diameter hexagonal puck, 3D-printed in yellow-and-black stripe pattern
- **Pushrod:** ¾" wooden dowel, 8" long, attached to the back of each puck. Slides through a bushing in the backboard
- **Spring:** Compression spring (3" long, ~15 lb force) on each pushrod, behind the backboard. Default position: bumper retracted flush with backboard
- **Cam mechanism:** Single cam axle runs horizontally behind the board, driven by the beekeeper's crank via a secondary belt/chain. Three cam lobes at 120° offset push the three pushrods in sequence
- **Cam lobes:** 3D-printed, bolted to a ¾" steel rod (cam axle). Each lobe pushes its pushrod ~2" forward (bumper pops out 2" into the playing field)
- **Return:** Compression springs pull bumpers back when cam lobe passes
- **Positions:**
  - Left field: X=14", Y=42" (guards Royal Jelly bucket)
  - Right field: X=34", Y=42" (mirrors left)
  - Above Queen: X=24", Y=54" (intercepts balls heading for spinner)

### 6. Beekeeper's Crank Station (back of board)

- **Handle:** 10" steel crank arm with rubber grip, mounted on a bearing on the back of the board at ~40" height (comfortable for standing adult)
- **Drive system:** Crank shaft drives TWO outputs:
  1. **Queen Spinner:** Timing belt to the spinner bearing. Gear ratio 3:1
  2. **Cam axle:** Secondary chain/belt to the bumper cam axle. Gear ratio 1:1 (one crank turn = one bumper sequence)
- **Tension adjustment:** Idler pulley on each belt for tension tuning
- **Visual:** The whole mechanism is visible from behind — parents lean around to watch the gears turn. It's part of the show

### 7. Scoring: Honey Pot Buckets (×7)

- **Shape:** Hexagonal buckets, 3D-printed in sections, 4" deep
- **Mounting:** Press-fit into hexagonal cutouts in the bottom edge rail
- **Drainage:** Each bucket has a ¾" hole in the back wall. Scored balls roll through into a rear collection chute
- **Candy tubes:** ¾" PVC tubes run from behind each bucket to a candy hopper at the back of the board. Gravity-fed. Each ball that scores pushes a candy portion forward into a dispensing tray at the player station
- **Dispensing mechanism:** Simple weight-activated gate — the ball's weight opens a gate, candy drops. Ball continues to the return chute. No electronics needed
- **Colors & labeling:**

| Bucket | Color | Print Material | Width |
|--------|-------|---------------|-------|
| QUEEN'S CELL (center) | Metallic gold + crown | Gold PLA | 2.5" |
| ROYAL JELLY (×2) | Deep purple-gold | Purple PLA + gold paint | 3.5" |
| HONEYCOMB (×2) | Classic gold | Gold PLA | 4.5" |
| POLLEN (×2) | Light yellow | Yellow PLA | 5.5" |
| THE STING (×2, edges) | Black + yellow stripes | Black PLA + yellow stripes | 2" |

### 8. Ball Return System

- **Collection chute:** Angled channel behind the backboard catches balls from all buckets + bottom gutter
- **Gravity feed:** Chute angles down to each player's ball magazine, with a center divider sending balls to the correct side
- **Magazine:** Vertical PVC tube (2" diameter) holding 10 balls stacked. Bottom ball drops into the launch chamber. Transparent tube so kids can see their ammo count

### 9. Sensory Systems

#### 9a. Visual — Blacklight & Fog
- **UV strip:** 24" UV LED strip mounted under a canopy at the top of the board. Aimed downward into the playing field
- **Fog machine:** Small 400W fog machine mounted behind the top of the board. Blows golden-tinted fog (fog fluid + gold-tinted glycol) across the top ⅓ of the field
- **Fog containment:** A small acrylic lip at the top of the front panel traps fog in the playing field rather than dispersing into the air
- **UV-reactive balls:** Standard yellow whiffle balls sprayed with UV-reactive clear coat. They GLOW in the fog zone

#### 9b. Audio — The Hive Hum
- **Bass transducer:** Small tactile transducer (Dayton Audio TT25-8, ~$10) bolted to the center of the backboard. Driven by a cheap Bluetooth amp playing a 200Hz sine wave loop. Creates a constant, felt hum — the sound of a beehive
- **Brass bell:** Mounted at the top of the board frame. Beekeeper rings it manually on Queen's Cell jackpot hits
- **Optional:** Small Bluetooth speaker playing honeybee ambiance (buzzing, nature sounds) at low volume

#### 9c. Scent — The Hive Smell
- **Cedar pegs:** Every 3rd row of pegs is cedar wood. Natural scent release
- **Beeswax melt:** Small candle warmer behind the board with a beeswax melt cube. Warm honey-wax scent drifts through the playing field via vent holes in the backboard
- **Backup:** A few drops of beeswax essential oil on cotton balls tucked into the edge rails

#### 9d. Touch — Mist Jets
- **Mister nozzles (×2):** Garden mister heads mounted on the frame, aimed at each player station
- **Water supply:** Gravity-fed from a 1-gallon jug mounted on the back of the frame
- **Activation:** Manual — beekeeper opens a ball valve for 1 second when a player hits "The Sting" gutter. Gets 'em with a quick mist spray. On hot days, it's a feature, not a punishment

### 10. Signage & Branding

- **Header sign:** "THE SWARM" in chunky gold block letters on black honeycomb background. 48" wide × 12" tall, mounted above the board
- **Bee mascot:** 3D-printed Grattan bee (12" tall) mounted on top of the header sign. Wings are separate pieces that wobble in the wind
- **Scoreboard:** Honeycomb grid on a chalkboard to the side. Write in scores with gold chalk markers
- **Beekeeper costume:** Bee-print hat, mesh veil, yellow work gloves. The whole outfit. $15 from Amazon

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Frame & Board (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut plywood backboard to 48×72"
2. Paint matte black base coat. Let dry
3. Stencil honeycomb hex pattern in metallic gold (30% opacity — subtle background texture)
4. Cut edge rails from 1×6, attach to plywood edges with screws
5. Mark and drill hex grid peg holes — 4.5" spacing, offset rows, ~90 positions
6. Cut 12" diameter hole for Queen Bee Spinner (center, 36" height). Reinforce with plywood ring on back
7. Cut three 6" diameter holes for bee bumpers at marked positions
8. Cut seven hexagonal openings in bottom edge rail for scoring buckets
9. Drill ¾" candy tube pass-through holes behind each bucket position
10. Build A-frame rear legs with hinges, cross brace, chain angle limiter
11. Mount rope handles on sides
12. Label all wing-nut joints for future reassembly

### Phase 2: Pegs, Acrylic & Scoring (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Insert ¾" dowels (mix of wood + cedar) through peg holes — 5" long, spanning full field depth
2. Press-fit 3D-printed hex caps onto front end of each dowel
3. Press-fit 3D-printed Honey Pot buckets into bottom rail cutouts
4. Build ball collection chute behind the backboard (angled plywood channel)
5. Run ¾" PVC candy tubes from behind each bucket to the candy hopper area
6. Drill acrylic front panel to match all peg holes + Queen cutout + bumper windows
7. DRY FIT acrylic (don't seal yet — need access for Phase 3 & 4)
8. Test ball flow through pegs by dropping balls from top — verify hex bounce pattern is chaotic and nothing snags

### Phase 3: Stomp Pad Launchers (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Cut two 24" diameter plywood circles (stomp pad tops)
2. Cut matching 24" plywood circles (stomp pad bottoms)
3. Cut and attach flower petal decorations (plywood or 3D-printed)
4. Sandwich rubber bladders between top and bottom plates
5. Attach piano hinge along back edge of each pad
6. Build 2×4 base frames for each pad
7. Mount rubber mats on stomp surfaces
8. Run 2" flex hose from each bladder to launch tube positions at the board
9. Build launch tubes: 1.5" PVC, 18" long, angled 70° at bottom corners of the board
10. Build ball magazines: 2" transparent PVC tubes, vertical, above each launch tube. Gravity-fed
11. Install rubber check valves at bladder outlets
12. **Test cycle:** Stomp → air burst → ball launches from tube → enters field at the right height
13. Calibrate: adjust bladder volume, hose length, tube angle until medium stomp = mid-board, monster stomp = top

### Phase 4: Queen Bee Spinner + Bumpers (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Assemble Queen Bee Spinner from 3D-printed sections. Insert internal baffles
2. Mount lazy susan bearing on backboard mounting plate, aligned with 12" cutout
3. Bolt spinner drum to bearing. Verify smooth rotation and that entry/exit slots align with the playing field gap
4. Mount ¾" steel cam axle on bearing brackets behind the backboard
5. Bolt three 3D-printed cam lobes onto axle at 120° offsets
6. Assemble bumper units: puck → pushrod → through bushing → compression spring on back side
7. Install all three bumper assemblies in their 6" holes
8. Mount crank handle bearing on back of board at 40" height
9. Run timing belt from crank to Queen Spinner (3:1 ratio)
10. Run chain/belt from crank to cam axle (1:1 ratio)
11. Install idler pulleys, adjust belt tension
12. **Test cycle:** Crank handle → Queen spins + bumpers pop in sequence. Verify bumpers retract cleanly. Verify Queen slots align with ball paths. Drop balls through the system
13. Tune: adjust cam lobe profile if bumper pop is too weak/strong. Adjust belt tension if Queen spins too fast/slow at comfortable crank speed

### Phase 5: Sensory Systems (Weekend 3, Day 1)
1. Mount UV LED strip under top canopy bracket
2. Mount fog machine behind top of board, aimed into playing field
3. Build small acrylic lip at top of front panel to contain fog
4. Bolt bass transducer to center of backboard. Wire to Bluetooth amp
5. Load 200Hz sine wave + BZZZT sound effects onto amp source (phone/MP3)
6. Mount brass bell at top of frame with pull cord accessible to beekeeper
7. Set up beeswax candle warmer behind the board
8. Mount mister nozzles on frame aimed at player stations
9. Mount 1-gallon water reservoir on back of frame, connect to misters with tubing + ball valve
10. Spray all whiffle balls with UV-reactive clear coat. Let dry 24 hrs
11. Spray hex peg caps with UV-reactive clear coat

### Phase 6: Final Assembly & Testing (Weekend 3, Day 2)
1. Mount acrylic front panel — bolt through all peg holes and edge rail
2. Seal all edges with weather stripping — no ball escape
3. Mount "THE SWARM" header sign with 3D-printed letters + bee mascot
4. Paint stomp pads in flower colors
5. Build and paint scoreboard (chalkboard paint on plywood, hex border)
6. Load candy into hoppers behind scoring buckets
7. Load whiffle balls into magazines
8. Set up beekeeper costume
9. **Full play test:**
   - Both stomp pads fire correctly, balls reach all zones
   - Queen Spinner catches and ejects balls reliably
   - Bumpers pop with satisfying force, don't jam
   - Candy dispenses on score
   - Fog fills top zone without overflowing
   - UV balls glow under blacklight
   - Bass hum is felt, not heard from 20ft away
   - Brass bell is audible from 50ft
   - Ball return feeds magazines reliably
   - Score all 20 balls without any jams or escapes
10. **Stress test:** 50 rapid-fire balls, fast cranking, monster stomps. Find the failure points. Fix them
11. **Pack test:** Disassemble to flat-pack, time it. Reassemble, time it. Target: 30 min setup, 15 min teardown

## Maintenance & Carnival Day Prep

### Pre-Carnival Checklist
- [ ] Test all stomp pad bladders for leaks (inflate, hold, check)
- [ ] Verify all compression springs (bumpers) still have snap
- [ ] Spin Queen Bee Spinner — smooth rotation, no binding
- [ ] Check all candy tubes are loaded and flowing
- [ ] Fresh batteries/charge in Bluetooth amp
- [ ] Fresh fog fluid
- [ ] Fresh beeswax melt cube
- [ ] Fill mister reservoir
- [ ] 24 UV whiffle balls present and glowing under UV
- [ ] Brass bell rings clean
- [ ] Beekeeper costume ready

### Spare Parts Kit (bring to carnival)
- 6 spare hex peg caps (3D printed)
- 3 spare compression springs
- 6 spare whiffle balls (UV coated)
- 1 spare rubber bladder
- Duct tape, zip ties, wood screws
- Extra fog fluid
- Extra candy

## Open Questions
- [ ] Power at the booth? Need outlets for: fog machine (400W), UV strip (5W), Bluetooth amp (10W), candle warmer (20W). Total: ~450W. One standard outlet + power strip
- [ ] Fog fluid gold tint — test with food coloring in fog fluid. If it stains, switch to standard white fog (still looks great under UV)
- [ ] Bladder durability — how many stomps before replacement? Test with 500 stomps before carnival. Have spare bladders ready
- [ ] Candy dispensing reliability — the weight-activated gate is the riskiest mechanism. Test extensively. Backup plan: beekeeper manually drops candy from behind the board on each score
- [ ] King-size bar chute — the Queen's Cell candy chute needs to be wide enough for a full-size bar. May need a separate, larger chute
