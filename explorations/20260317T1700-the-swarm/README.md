# THE SWARM

## Concept

Two players stand on giant 3D-printed FLOWER PADS and STOMP to launch glowing pollen balls upward into a 4×6 ft honeycomb plinko field — the biggest, most bee-infested game board in carnival history. Balls rocket up through hex-peg hive cells, ricochet off spinning DRONE BEE wheels, get swallowed by the central QUEEN BEE SPINNER (a rotating hex drum that ejects balls from one of six random exits), and cascade down through a fog of golden "pollen cloud" into HONEY POT scoring buckets that dispense honey sticks on every score.

A volunteer BEEKEEPER behind the board cranks a handle that simultaneously powers the Queen Bee Spinner AND three spring-loaded BEE BUMPERS that pop out of the honeycomb, smacking balls in wild new directions. The harder the beekeeper cranks, the faster the spinner turns and the more violently the bumpers fire. The beekeeper IS the difficulty dial.

The whole board buzzes. Literally — a bass transducer behind the backboard hums at 200Hz (the resonant frequency of a beehive). Cedar-and-beeswax scent wafts from the peg field. UV-reactive pollen balls glow under a blacklight canopy. When someone hits the QUEEN'S CELL jackpot, a brass bell rings, golden fog erupts from the top, and the bass drops a thunderous BZZZZZT that you feel in your teeth.

This is Buzz Ball reborn — with stomp pads, sensory overload, a rotating queen mechanism, simultaneous two-player chaos, and candy on every hit. Every element screams Grattan honeybee. Every kid leaves buzzing.

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×6 ft honeycomb board tilted at ~20° from vertical, peaking at ~7ft
- Two FLOWER PAD stomp stations on the ground, one per player, each connected to a pneumatic launch tube via flex hose
- Hex peg field: 3D-printed hexagonal caps on dowel pegs, arranged in true hex grid (not square — six-directional chaos)
- Central QUEEN BEE SPINNER: 10" diameter rotating hex drum, crank-powered from behind
- Three BEE BUMPERS: spring-loaded hex pucks on the same crank mechanism
- UV blacklight strip across the top canopy
- Small fog machine behind the crown (golden-tinted fog fluid)
- Bass transducer bolted to backboard
- Clear acrylic front panel
- 10 UV-reactive yellow whiffle balls per player ("pollen balls"), gravity-fed from a magazine

### The Stomp

Each player's station is a 24" diameter flower-shaped platform (plywood, painted in bright petals — red, purple, orange) over a heavy-duty air bladder. STOMP the flower, air blasts through a flex hose to the launch tube, and a pollen ball rockets upward into the hive.

- **Light stomp** → ball enters lower third of the hex field. Short cascade, predictable, safer scores
- **MONSTER STOMP** → ball rockets to the top, enters the fog zone, bounces through the full hex field. Maximum chaos. Access to the Queen Bee Spinner. Risk of gutters
- **Rapid stomping** → balls auto-feed from the magazine. Stomp-stomp-stomp sends a swarm of balls into the field simultaneously. 5+ balls bouncing through hex pegs at once is MESMERIZING
- **Dry stomp** (stomp before next ball loads) → satisfying WHOMP of air, no ball. The crowd laughs

### The Queen Bee Spinner

Dead center of the board: a 10" diameter hexagonal drum on a bearing, with 6 entry/exit slots around its perimeter. When a ball enters any slot, it rattles around inside the drum and ejects from whichever slot happens to be facing down when it exits. The drum's rotation speed is controlled by the beekeeper's crank.

- **Slow crank** → balls enter and exit predictably. Drama. Suspense. Which slot will it choose?
- **Fast crank** → balls get WHIPPED around and flung out at speed. Total chaos. Could go anywhere
- **No crank** → drum is stationary, balls pass straight through. Boring. The beekeeper would NEVER

The Queen Bee Spinner is the heart of the game. It's the moment where the crowd holds its breath. A ball enters the Queen's domain and ANYTHING can happen.

### The Bee Bumpers (×3)

Three 5" hexagonal pucks in yellow-and-black stripes, mounted on spring-loaded rods behind the backboard. Connected to the beekeeper's crank via a cam mechanism (120° offset — bumpers fire in sequence as the crank turns). When a bumper fires, it punches out ~2" from the board face, SMACKING any ball that's nearby.

The bumpers are positioned:
- **Left field** — guards the high-value honey pots
- **Right field** — mirrors the left
- **Above the Queen** — intercepts balls heading for the spinner, creating a "do I go for the Queen or play it safe?" decision

### Scoring: The Honey Pots

Seven hexagonal buckets at the bottom of the board, shaped like honeycomb cells, each with a gravity-fed honey stick tube behind it.

| Bucket | Points | Width | Candy | Visual |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|--------|
| **QUEEN'S CELL** | 1000 | 2.5" | Full honey dipper + king-size candy | Gold hex, tiny opening, crown painted above |
| **ROYAL JELLY (×2)** | 300 | 3.5" | Honey stick + fun-size bar | Deep purple-gold hex cells |
| **HONEYCOMB (×2)** | 150 | 4.5" | Honey stick | Classic gold hex cells |
| **POLLEN (×2)** | 50 | 5.5" | Single gummy bear | Light yellow hex cells |
| **THE STING (×2)** | -25 | 2" | Nothing. Sad buzz. | Black hex gutters on edges — you got STUNG |

### Special Mechanics

- **SWARM BONUS:** If a ball hits the Queen Bee Spinner AND a Bee Bumper in the same run, points are DOUBLED. The ball "joined the swarm"
- **POLLINATION COMBO:** If both players' balls are inside the Queen Bee Spinner at the same time, BOTH balls score double when they exit. Cross-pollination!
- **QUEEN'S CELL JACKPOT:** Hit the 2.5" center bucket → brass bell rings, golden fog erupts, bass drops the BZZZT, beekeeper does a victory dance, every kid in line loses their mind
- **HIVE MIND MODE (co-op):** Both players same team. Combined score vs. target thresholds:
  - 🐝 **Worker Bee:** 2,000 pts — "You fed the hive!"
  - 🐝🐝 **Drone:** 4,000 pts — "Hive protector!"
  - 👑 **QUEEN BEE:** 8,000 pts — "LONG LIVE THE QUEEN!" (bell rings, fog erupts, legendary status)

### Round Structure
- **60-second rounds.** Both players stomp simultaneously. As fast as you can
- 10 pollen balls per player, auto-feeding from magazine
- Balls from BOTH players share the same hex field, Queen Spinner, and bumpers — cross-player interactions are constant
- Score tallied at the end. Beekeeper announces winner (or co-op threshold)
- **Speed Bonus:** +200 pts for launching all 10 before time expires

## What Makes It Special

1. **THE STOMP.** Full-body flower-pad stomping is leagues beyond pulling a plunger. Kids are jumping, slamming, going feral on a giant flower. The line watches players bouncing up and down like they're on a trampoline. Parents get a free 60-second cardio break for their kid

2. **THE QUEEN BEE SPINNER.** Nothing like it at any carnival anywhere. A rotating hex drum that swallows balls and spits them out randomly. It's a roulette wheel embedded in a plinko board. The crowd watches a ball enter and holds its breath. Where will the Queen send it? The drama is ELECTRIC

3. **SIMULTANEOUS MULTI-BALL CHAOS.** Both players stomping at once means 5-8 balls bouncing through the hex field simultaneously. Balls collide. Balls knock each other into the Queen. Your pollen ball bumps their pollen ball into the jackpot. The interactions are emergent and unpredictable. This is SWARM energy

4. **THE BEEKEEPER.** A volunteer in a bee hat and veil cranking chaos into the board. They control the Queen's speed AND the bumper timing. They can crank fast for pandemonium or slow for drama. They become a character — the villain, the hero, the chaos agent. Kids love them. Kids hate them. Kids remember them

5. **FIVE SENSES IN THE HIVE.**
   - 👁️ **SIGHT:** UV-reactive pollen balls glowing through golden fog, hex pegs in Grattan gold-and-black, spinning Queen drum
   - 👂 **SOUND:** Bass hum of the hive (200Hz), THWOMP of stomp pads, clatter of hex pegs, brass bell on jackpot, BZZZT bass drop
   - ✋ **TOUCH:** Full-body stomp impact, bass vibration through the frame, mist spray on "Sting" gutter hits
   - 👃 **SMELL:** Cedar pegs + beeswax oil diffuser. The booth smells like the inside of a real hive. The smell triggers the memory 5 years later
   - 👅 **TASTE:** Honey sticks and candy from every scoring bucket. Kids walk away tasting honey. The Queen's Cell drops a king-size bar

6. **THE AESTHETIC.** Every surface is hexagonal. The pegs are hexes. The buckets are hexes. The Queen is a hex drum. The bumpers are hex pucks. The flower stomp pads have hex centers. The scoreboard is a honeycomb grid. It's 4×6 feet of unmistakable Grattan gold-and-black honeycomb, visible from across the schoolyard. It IS the carnival

7. **CANDY ON EVERY SCORE.** Not tickets. Not points on a screen. A honey stick drops into your hand the moment your ball scores. The dopamine loop is INSTANT. Even the 50-point Pollen bucket drops a gummy bear. The only punishment is The Sting — zero candy, sad buzz, mist spray to the face. Which is honestly also a reward on a hot day

8. **GRATTAN DNA.** This isn't a generic carnival game with bee stickers slapped on it. The honeycomb IS the game mechanic (hex pegs = 6-directional chaos). The bee bumpers ARE the twist. The Queen Bee Spinner IS the drama. The Beekeeper IS the operator. Every design decision flows from "what would a beehive do?" This game could only exist at Grattan. Years from now: *"Remember THE SWARM?"*
