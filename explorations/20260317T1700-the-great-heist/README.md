# THE GREAT HEIST

## Concept
A giant 4ft × 3ft tilting labyrinth table. The surface is a bank vault floor plan — laser grids (string), security cameras (bumpers), guard patrol routes (channels), and a VAULT at the center. Players grip two hand cranks — one tilts the table left/right, the other tilts forward/back — to guide a steel ball bearing from the ENTRANCE through the maze to the VAULT without dropping into trap holes (alarm triggers). It's timed. Beat the vault in under 60 seconds? You're a master thief. Drop into a trap? Alarm sounds (bicycle horn mounted underneath), start over from the entrance.

This is the wooden labyrinth game everyone played as a kid, blown up to 4 feet wide, themed as a heist movie, and turned into a spectator sport.

## The Pitch
*"You're breaking into the vault! Tilt the table to guide your ball through the security maze. Don't fall in the traps — that's the alarm! Make it to the vault before time runs out. Steady hands only. Ready? The heist begins... NOW!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Labyrinth table: 48"W × 36"D playing surface, mounted on a gimbal frame at waist height (30")
- Two hand cranks on the front edge — left crank tilts left/right, right crank tilts forward/back
- Steel ball bearing: 1" diameter (heavy enough to feel the tilt, visible from spectator distance)
- Maze walls: ¼" plywood strips (1.5" tall) glued to the surface, creating the bank floor plan
- Trap holes: 1.5" diameter holes cut in the surface (ball falls through, collected in a tray underneath)
- 60-second sand timer mounted on the frame (or phone timer)
- Bicycle horn under the table — connected to trap holes via a simple lever/flap mechanism (ball drops, hits flap, honk!)

### The Maze
The playing surface is painted as a top-down bank vault floor plan. The maze walls ARE the bank's walls, corridors, and security barriers.

### Difficulty Tiers
The maze has THREE paths from entrance to vault, each with different difficulty:

| Path | Route | Traps | Time Needed | Nickname |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Easy Street | Wide corridors, gentle turns | 2 traps | ~45 sec | "The Rookie Job" |
| Side Hustle | Medium corridors, tight turns | 4 traps | ~30 sec | "The Pro Job" |
| Air Duct | Narrow passages, sharp angles | 6 traps | ~20 sec | "The Impossible Job" |

Players naturally discover they can choose their route. Beginners take Easy Street. Veterans try the Air Duct. The Impossible Job in under 20 seconds is genuine bragging rights.

### Scoring
- **Vault Reached (any path):** WIN — you cracked the vault!
- **Speed Bonus:** Under 30 sec = Silver Badge, Under 20 sec = Gold Badge, Under 15 sec = Diamond Badge
- **Clean Heist:** Vault reached without triggering any alarms (ball never entered a trap) = Bonus prize
- **Alarm = Reset:** Ball falls in a trap → HONK → ball returns to entrance tray, try again (clock keeps running)

### The Cranks
- Two hand cranks mounted on the front rail of the table frame
- Left crank: connected via cable to the table's left/right tilt axis. Turn clockwise = tilt right. Counterclockwise = tilt left
- Right crank: connected via cable to the forward/back tilt axis
- Tilt range: ±5° in each direction (enough to roll the ball but not violent)
- Cranks provide proportional control — small turns = gentle tilt, big turns = steep tilt
- The dual-crank mechanic requires BOTH HANDS working independently. This is the core skill — coordinating two axes simultaneously. It's like patting your head and rubbing your belly, but with immediate visual feedback

### Trap Holes
- 8 total trap holes spread across the maze (2 easy path, 4 medium, 6 hard — some shared)
- Each hole has a small hinged flap underneath
- When the ball drops through, it hits the flap, which triggers a bicycle horn via a simple lever linkage
- Ball drops into a foam-lined collection tray, rolls to a return slot at the front
- Player grabs the ball, places it back at the entrance, continues (clock still running)

## What Makes It Special

1. **PURE SKILL, ZERO LUCK** — This is the most skill-intensive game possible. No randomness. No dice, no bouncing, no cascade. Your hands control the outcome. Period. The ball goes exactly where you tilt it. Every failure is YOUR fault. Every success is YOUR achievement. This creates genuine mastery motivation — kids who fail want to try again IMMEDIATELY because they know they can do better.

2. **THE TWO-HAND CHALLENGE** — Coordinating two cranks (two tilt axes) simultaneously is a unique motor skill challenge. It's intuitive within seconds but takes real practice to master. Watching a kid's brain click when they figure out how to smoothly coordinate both cranks is magic. It's like learning to drive a stick shift — frustrating for 30 seconds, then incredibly satisfying.

3. **SPECTATOR TENSION** — A crowd gathers around the table watching the ball roll through the maze. It approaches a trap... the player's hands tremble on the cranks... the ball teeters on the edge... HONK! Groans from the crowd. Or: the ball threads past the trap by a millimeter and the crowd erupts. This game creates HOLDING-YOUR-BREATH moments that other carnival games don't. It's a spectator sport.

4. **THE HEIST NARRATIVE** — "Break into the vault" is instantly understood and instantly exciting. The bank floor plan theme transforms a tilting maze into a MISSION. Kids aren't just rolling a ball — they're dodging lasers, sneaking past guards, cracking the vault. The narrative makes every trap hole feel consequential ("the alarm went off!") and every success feel heroic ("I made it to the vault!").

5. **THREE PATHS = INFINITE REPLAY** — First-timers take Easy Street. After they crack the vault, they come back for the Side Hustle. Masters attempt the Air Duct. The same physical game has three distinct difficulty levels built into the maze layout. No adjustments needed.

6. **THE HORN** — The bicycle horn alarm is comedy gold. The moment of silence as the ball teeters... then HONK. Kids and parents burst out laughing every single time. The horn is the game's signature sound effect — you'll hear it across the carnival and know someone just triggered the alarm.

7. **EASIEST BUILD IN THE LINEUP** — A plywood surface with wood strip walls glued on, mounted on a gimbal (two dowel axes with bearings). Two cranks, some cable, a bicycle horn. No acrylic panels, no 3D printing required, no electronics, no paint complexity (though the heist theme painting is optional fun). A competent builder could knock this out in a single weekend.
