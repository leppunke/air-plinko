# EARTHQUAKE! — Build Plan

## Concept
Cooperative tilt-plinko. Two players stand on footplates connected to a rocker beam. Their weight shifts tilt the entire plinko board left or right. Balls drop from a hopper at the top and cascade through pegs — where they land depends on how the two players coordinate their balance. Scoring zones at the bottom reward centered, balanced play. Gutters punish over-tilting.

## Architecture

### 1. The Plinko Board (Tilting Assembly)

#### Back Panel
- 4×5 ft sheet of ¾" plywood
- Painted matte black with neon zone markings (spray paint)
- Peg holes drilled in offset grid pattern, ~5" spacing, 10 columns × 11 rows (~110 pegs)

#### Front Panel
- 4×5 ft clear acrylic sheet, ¼" thick
- Drilled to match peg hole pattern
- Mounted to edge rails with wing nuts for easy removal (ball jams, maintenance)

#### Edge Rails
- 1×4 lumber on all four sides, sandwiched between plywood and acrylic
- Creates the 4" enclosed playing field
- Bottom rail has 9 openings cut for scoring slots (see Scoring Zones)

#### Pegs
- ¾" wooden dowels, 3.5" long
- Inserted through acrylic, glued into plywood
- Painted in alternating neon colors: hot pink, electric green, bright yellow, cyan
- Mushroom-cap tips (small wooden ball glued to each peg tip) — prevents balls from resting on top

### 2. The Pivot System

#### Central Axle
- 1" steel pipe, 54" long (extends 3" past each side of the board)
- Passes through the board frame at the vertical midpoint (30" up from the bottom)
- Board hangs on this axle and tilts freely left-right

#### Axle Mounts
- Two heavy-duty pillow block bearings (1" bore, flange-mount)
- Bolted to the tops of the support frame uprights
- Allows smooth, low-friction rotation

#### Tilt Limiters
- Two rubber bumper stops mounted on the support frame
- Contact the board frame at ±8° tilt
- Prevents over-rotation — boards stays within safe, playable range

### 3. Support Frame (Non-Tilting)

#### Uprights (×2)
- 4×4 posts, 66" tall
- Anchored to a base platform
- Pillow block bearings mounted at 30" height on inside faces

#### Base Platform
- 2×6 lumber frame, 72"W × 36"D
- Plywood deck for stability
- The rocker beam sits ON TOP of this platform

#### Cross Braces
- 2×4 horizontals connecting the uprights at top and bottom
- Keeps the frame rigid while the board swings

#### Rear Stabilizer Legs
- Two angled 2×4 legs extending rearward from the uprights
- Prevents the whole structure from tipping toward players

### 4. The Rocker Platform (Player Input)

#### Rocker Beam
- 6 ft length of 4×4 lumber
- Sits on a center fulcrum (steel pipe or rounded hardwood block bolted to the base platform)
- Extends 12" past each side of the board

#### Fulcrum
- 1.5" steel pipe welded/bolted to a base plate, mounted to center of the base platform
- Rocker beam has a matching channel routed into its bottom to sit on the pipe
- Allows smooth rocking left-right

#### Footplates (×2)
- 18" × 24" plywood pads mounted to each end of the rocker beam
- Covered in rubber matting (anti-slip)
- Painted with footprint outlines — "STAND HERE"
- Low side rails (2" tall) so kids don't step off accidentally

#### Linkage: Rocker → Board Tilt
- Two rigid steel rods (½" threaded rod, ~30" long)
- Connect from each end of the rocker beam UP to the bottom edge of the tilting board
- When the rocker tilts left, the left rod pushes the board left, right rod pulls — board tilts left
- Rod ends use rod-end bearings (heim joints) for smooth pivoting
- Adjustment: heim joints thread onto the rod, so effective length is tunable — calibrate tilt ratio

### 5. Ball Hopper & Drop System

#### Hopper
- Plywood box, 12"W × 8"D × 10"H, mounted to the top-center of the board
- Open top for loading balls
- Holds 20 whiffle balls
- Gravity-fed: balls roll down an internal ramp to a release gate

#### Release Gate
- Simple rotating drum with a single ball-sized notch (3" hole in a 6" diameter wooden cylinder)
- Drum rotates via a hand crank on the side (operated by a game attendant) — one crank = one ball drops
- Alternative: motorized rotation (battery drill with a speed controller) for consistent timing
- Ball drops through a hole in the top edge rail into the playing field at top-center

### 6. Scoring Zones (Bottom of Board)

#### Slot Layout (left to right)
| Slot | Label | Width | Points |
|------|-------|-------|--------|
| 1 | GUTTER | 3" | 0 |
| 2 | 25 | 5" | 25 |
| 3 | 50 | 5" | 50 |
| 4 | 100 | 5" | 100 |
| 5 | ★ JACKPOT ★ | 3" | 500 |
| 6 | 100 | 5" | 100 |
| 7 | 50 | 5" | 50 |
| 8 | 25 | 5" | 25 |
| 9 | GUTTER | 3" | 0 |

Total width: 41" (fits within the 48" board with 3.5" edge rails on each side)

#### Construction
- Dividers: 4" tall strips of ¼" plywood, glued/screwed vertically to the back panel
- Each slot funnels into a collection tray behind the board
- Trays are angled to drain balls into a single collection bin on the ground behind the game

#### Labeling
- Point values painted in large neon numbers on the back panel above each slot
- JACKPOT slot lit with a small battery-powered LED strip (pulsing gold)
- GUTTER slots painted red with "×0" markers

### 7. Sound Effects (Optional but Recommended)

- **Gutter buzzer:** Piezo buzzer + tilt switch in each gutter tray — ball weight triggers it
- **Gold ball bell:** Attendant rings a hand bell before dropping a gold ball
- **Richter 10 bell:** Mounted ship's bell or large dinner triangle — attendant rings if team hits 5,000+
- Total cost for sound: ~$15 (2 buzzers + hand bell)

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Support Frame & Rocker (Weekend 1)

**Reality check:** This project has a precision mechanical linkage system (pillow blocks, steel axle, heim joints, rocker-to-board calibration). Unlike a standard plinko board, you cannot eyeball the alignment — the pillow block bearings must be perfectly co-linear or the board will bind. The heim joint linkage needs iterative length adjustment to get the tilt ratio right. **Budget 4–5 weekends total, not 2.** The frame and rocker alone are a full weekend when you include the fulcrum routing and rocker balance testing.

1. Cut 4×4 uprights to 66" each
2. Build base platform from 2×6 lumber — 72"W × 36"D rectangle with cross-members
3. Bolt uprights to base platform (inside, 48" apart center-to-center)
4. Add rear stabilizer legs (angled 2×4s from uprights to back of base platform)
5. Add cross braces between uprights at top and at 12" height
6. Mount fulcrum pipe to center of base platform
7. Route channel in bottom of 4×4 rocker beam to sit on fulcrum
8. Cut and mount footplates to each end of rocker beam — add rubber matting
9. Set rocker beam on fulcrum — test rocking motion (should be smooth, centered)
10. Add low side rails to footplates, paint footprint outlines

### Phase 2: Plinko Board (Weekend 1, Day 2)

1. Cut back panel plywood to 48×60"
2. Mark peg grid: offset rows, 5" spacing, ~10 columns × 11 rows
3. Drill ¾" peg holes through back panel
4. Cut edge rails from 1×4 — attach to all four sides of back panel
5. Cut scoring slot openings in bottom edge rail (9 slots per layout above)
6. Paint back panel matte black
7. Insert and glue dowel pegs — paint neon colors, add mushroom-cap tips
8. Paint neon scoring numbers and zone markers on back panel
9. Build scoring dividers from ¼" plywood strips, mount inside bottom section

### Phase 3: Pivot & Linkage (Weekend 3 — this is the hard weekend)

**This phase is the crux of the entire build.** The pillow block bearings must be mounted at exactly the same height on both uprights, or the board will bind/wobble. The linkage rod lengths need iterative adjustment — expect 2–3 hours of test-adjust-test cycles to get the tilt ratio feeling right. Don't rush this.

1. Mount pillow block bearings to inside faces of uprights at 30" height — **use a level and measure from the base platform, not from the top of the uprights** (wood posts are never perfectly identical)
2. Drill/mount board axle (1" pipe) through the board's edge rail frames at the 30" height line
3. Set board axle into pillow block bearings — board should hang and tilt freely. **If it binds, shim one bearing. Do not proceed until the board swings freely with zero binding.**
4. Mount rubber tilt limiters on uprights — test ±8° range
5. Install linkage rods: heim joint on rocker beam end → threaded rod → heim joint on board bottom rail
6. Calibrate: adjust heim joint threading until rocker tilt produces proportional board tilt. **This will take longer than you think — the geometry is non-linear, and small changes in rod length produce large changes in tilt ratio at the extremes.**
7. Full tilt test — step on each footplate, verify smooth, responsive tilting

### Phase 4: Hopper, Acrylic & Finish (Weekends 4–5)

1. Build ball hopper box, mount to top-center of board
2. Build release drum (wooden cylinder with ball-sized notch, mounted on a shaft through hopper)
3. Attach hand crank to release drum shaft
4. Drill acrylic to match peg holes
5. Mount acrylic front panel with wing nuts on edge rails (removable for maintenance)
6. Build collection trays behind scoring slots — angle to drain into ground-level bin
7. Install LED strip on jackpot slot, wire buzzer in gutter trays
8. Load 20 balls into hopper, full playtest
9. Calibrate: adjust peg positions if balls consistently avoid certain scoring zones
10. Add vinyl lettering: "EARTHQUAKE!" header, scoring labels, "STAND HERE" on footplates
11. Attach handles to back panel sides for transport

## Transport & Storage

- Board lifts off axle bearings (two people, ~40 lbs)
- Rocker beam lifts off fulcrum
- Stabilizer legs detach with wing nuts
- Uprights detach from base platform with carriage bolts
- Flat-packs into: 1 board (4×5 ft), 1 base platform (6×3 ft), 2 uprights, 1 rocker beam, hardware bag
- Two-person carry with gate handles
- Reassembly: ~30 minutes with no tools (all wing nut / carriage bolt joints)

---

## Safety Notes

- Footplate side rails prevent stepping off during play
- Rubber bumper stops limit tilt to safe range (±8°)
- Rear stabilizer legs prevent tip-over even under asymmetric loading
- Acrylic front panel prevents balls from escaping forward
- Base platform wide enough (36" deep) to keep center of gravity well within footprint
- Rubber matting on footplates for grip
- Max recommended player weight: 150 lbs per side (4×4 rocker beam rated well beyond)
