# EARTHQUAKE!

## Concept
A cooperative full-body plinko game where two players stand on opposite ends of a giant seesaw platform that tilts the entire plinko board. Balls drop from the top and cascade through pegs — but WHERE they land depends on both players shifting their weight together to tilt the board left, right, or keep it level. One kid can't do it alone. It takes two bodies, constant communication, and split-second coordination to steer balls into the high-value scoring zones.

The board is alive. The floor is moving. The balls are bouncing. And two kids are screaming "LEFT! LEFT! NO, MY LEFT!" at each other while a crowd watches balls cascade through a rainbow of pegs on a swaying board. That's a core memory.

## The Pitch
*"The ground is SHAKING! Stand on the earthquake platform, work with your partner, and tilt the board to catch every ball! Ready? The earthquake starts in THREE... TWO... ONE... RUMBLE!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×5 ft plinko board mounted on a central pivot point (tilts left-right up to ~8°)
- Two footplate stations — one on each side of the board's base, connected to the tilt platform
- A ball hopper at the top center drops one ball every ~3 seconds (20 balls per round, 60-second game)
- Clear acrylic front panel — spectators see everything

### Core Mechanic: The Tilt
- The board pivots on a central horizontal axle running across the middle of its width
- Two wooden footplates extend from the base, one left, one right — connected by a rocker beam
- When Player 1 (left) shifts weight onto their plate, the board tilts LEFT — balls cascade leftward through the pegs
- When Player 2 (right) shifts weight, the board tilts RIGHT
- When both balance evenly — board stays level, balls fall straight down the center
- **The magic:** neither player can tilt the board alone past ~4°. Full tilt requires BOTH players to commit — Player 1 steps ON while Player 2 steps BACK. Cooperative by physics.

### The Scoring Zones (Bottom of the Board)
```
 GUTTER | 25 | 50 | 100 | ★500★ | 100 | 50 | 25 | GUTTER
   ×0     ←   ←    ←    CENTER    →    →    →    ×0
                         JACKPOT
```

- **JACKPOT (center, 500 pts):** Tiny 3" slot. Board must be PERFECTLY LEVEL. Both players balanced. Requires zen-like coordination
- **100-pt zones (×2):** Flanking the jackpot. Slight tilt toward center. Rewarded for good-but-not-perfect teamwork
- **50-pt zones (×2):** Moderate tilt. Solid points, forgiving
- **25-pt zones (×2):** Heavy tilt. Easy to hit but low reward
- **GUTTERS (×2, edges):** Maximum tilt = balls fly off the side. Zero points + a loud buzzer. The crowd goes "OHHH!"

### The Twist: Combo Balls
- Every 5th ball dropped is a GOLD ball (painted gold, slightly heavier)
- Gold balls score DOUBLE in whatever zone they land
- When a gold ball drops, a bell rings — both players know to focus
- Creates dramatic moments: "GOLD BALL! HOLD STEADY! HOLD STEAAAADY!"

### Win Condition
- **Cooperative target thresholds** (combined score, both players win or lose together):
  - 🥉 **Tremor:** 1,000 pts — "You survived!"
  - 🥈 **Quake:** 2,500 pts — "Earthquake experts!"
  - 🥇 **Richter 10:** 5,000 pts — "LEGENDARY SHAKERS!"
- Scores displayed on a big hand-painted board next to the game
- Optional: ring a giant bell if anyone hits Richter 10. The whole carnival hears it.

## What Makes It Special

1. **Cooperation IS the mechanic** — not an optional mode, not a tacked-on variant. The game fundamentally cannot be played solo. Two kids must physically coordinate their bodies to control the board. Every single point is earned together.

2. **Full-body gameplay** — kids are shifting, leaning, stepping, balancing. It's physical. They feel it in their legs. It's like a balance board meets Dance Dance Revolution meets Plinko.

3. **Constant communication** — "TILT LEFT! MORE! TOO MUCH! BACK! HOLD IT! HOOOLD!" Kids who've never met are instantly teammates screaming instructions at each other. Friendships are forged in the earthquake.

4. **The tension curve** — early balls are practice. Mid-game they're finding rhythm. Gold ball drops and everything gets intense. Final 5 balls they're either in sync or in chaos. Every round tells a story.

5. **Spectator gold** — watching two kids on a wobble platform trying to balance while balls cascade through neon pegs is inherently hilarious. The board swaying, balls bouncing, kids screaming — it draws a crowd from 50 feet.

6. **Accessible difficulty** — kindergartners can play (just step on the plates and giggle while balls go everywhere). 5th graders strategize ("you step light, I'll lean hard, we go for the jackpot"). Same game, scales with age.

7. **The sound design** — the THUNK of balls hitting pegs on a tilting board. The bell for gold balls. The buzzer for gutters. The CLANG of the Richter 10 bell. This game has an audio presence.

8. **Stupidly simple to understand** — "Stand there. Tilt the board together. Catch the balls in the middle." 5-second explanation. Zero onboarding. Kids who've been watching in line already know how to play.

## Vibe
Think: trust fall meets Plinko meets standing on a surfboard with your best friend during an earthquake. Cooperative chaos. Coordinated panic. The kind of game where strangers become teammates and everyone in line is shouting advice. Kids will drag their parents back for "ONE MORE TRY, we almost got Richter 10!"
