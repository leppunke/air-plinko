# M18 Cyclone Plinko — Build Plan

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Board Width | 48" (4 ft) | Two scoring zones side-by-side |
| Board Height | 72" (6 ft) | Taller = more hang time = more blast opportunities |
| Depth (gap) | 5" | Whiffle ball clearance + airflow space |
| Tilt angle | ~10° from vertical | Slight tilt keeps balls moving down, gravity-assisted |
| Total footprint | 48"W × 30"D × 78"H | Including frame legs and player space |

---

## Architecture

### 1. Main Board & Frame

**Back panel:** 4×6 ft sheet of ¾" plywood, painted matte black. This is the backdrop — dark so balls and scoring zones pop visually.

**Front panel:** 4×6 ft clear acrylic sheet, ¼" thick. Players watch the action through this. Mounted with wing nuts for easy removal/maintenance.

**Edge rails:** 1×6 lumber on all four sides, sandwiched between plywood and acrylic. Creates the sealed 5"-deep playing field. Rails are painted Milwaukee Red (#DB0032).

**Frame legs:** Two rear A-frame legs made from 2×4 lumber, hinged at top for fold-flat storage. Cross brace at 30" height. Rubber feet. Chain angle limiters set the 10° tilt.

**Top header:** 1×8 lumber across the top, extends 6" above the board. Painted black with "CYCLONE PLINKO" in Milwaukee Red. M18 Tower Light mounts here as the booth beacon.

### 2. Peg Field

**Layout:** Offset grid pattern covering the middle 60% of the board (rows 12"–54" from bottom).

**Pegs:** ¾" wooden dowels, 4" long, drilled through acrylic into plywood. Serve as both spacers and deflectors.

**Spacing:** 5" horizontal, 4" vertical, offset rows (classic plinko stagger).

**Peg count:** Approximately 70–80 pegs across 10 rows.

**Top zone (54"–66"):** Open — no pegs. This is the DROP ZONE where balls enter and begin their descent. Three drop slots at top rail.

**Bottom zone (0"–12"):** Open — no pegs. This is the SCORING ZONE where balls land in pockets.

### 3. Drop System (Top)

**Three drop slots** cut into the top edge rail, evenly spaced at 12", 24", and 36" from left edge.

**Each slot:** 3" wide × 3" deep notch in the top rail, with a short curved ramp (bent aluminum or 3D-printed) that guides the ball cleanly into the playing field.

**Ball holder tray:** Small shelf mounted on the front of the top rail. Holds 5 balls per player. Players reach up to drop balls (or a carnival operator loads them).

**For shorter kids:** A step stool (built from 2×4 scraps, 12" high) on each side. Or an operator drops the ball for them from behind.

### 4. Air Port System — THE MAIN EVENT

**Six air ports total — three per side** at LOW (8"), MID (28"), and HIGH (48") measured from the bottom of the board.

**Each air port:**
- 3" diameter hole through the side edge rail
- Flared funnel entry (3D-printed or shaped from sheet metal) — guides the blower nozzle into the port
- Internal baffle: a 3"-wide channel through the edge rail that straightens airflow before entering the field
- Exit side: open into the playing field, directed horizontally across the board

**Port positioning:**
- LEFT side ports: aimed RIGHT across the field (Red player)
- RIGHT side ports: aimed LEFT across the field (Blue player)
- Slight upward angle (5°) on MID and HIGH ports to account for gravity

**Air port mounting detail:**
- Each funnel is mounted with two screws into the edge rail
- Funnel interior is smooth (sanded or lined with duct tape) so the blower nozzle slides in and out easily
- Rubber gasket ring around the funnel opening — seals against the blower nozzle tip for maximum air transfer
- Port is sized for the M18 Compact Blower nozzle tip (2.5" OD)

### 5. Scoring Pockets (Bottom)

**Seven pockets** built into the bottom zone, divided by angled wooden dividers.

**From left to right:**
| Pocket | Width | Points | Color |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| Gutter L | 4" | 0 | Black |
| LOW L | 6" | 10 | White |
| MID L | 5" | 25 | Yellow |
| HIGH L | 4" | 50 | Orange |
| JACKPOT | 3" | 100 | Milwaukee Red |
| HIGH R | 4" | 50 | Orange |
| MID R | 5" | 25 | Yellow |
| LOW R | 6" | 10 | White |
| Gutter R | 4" | 0 | Black |

**Dividers:** ¼" plywood strips, 4" tall, angled slightly inward at top to funnel balls in. Painted to match pocket color.

**Pocket floor:** Slightly angled toward the back so balls roll to a collection trough behind the board.

**Drainage:** 2" holes in the back panel behind each pocket. Balls drop through to a rear collection chute.

### 6. Ball Return System

**Rear chute:** Angled trough (V-shaped from two pieces of 1×4) running the full width of the board, mounted to the back at the bottom. Catches all balls from scoring pocket drainage.

**Center split:** Divider in the chute sends balls to left or right collection bin.

**Collection bins:** Two open-top bins (small bucket or box) at the base, one per side. Operator or players grab balls for the next round.

### 7. M18 Tool Mounting & Tethering

**Blower tethers (×2):**
- 4ft bungee cord with carabiner clip
- One end bolted to a steel eyebolt in the frame leg
- Other end clips to the blower's belt hook loop
- Allows full reach to all 3 air ports on player's side
- Cannot reach the other player's side or be aimed at people

**Battery station:**
- Horizontal 2×4 mounted across the rear cross brace
- 4 M18 battery clips (3D-printed or bought) mounted to it
- Holds spare batteries for quick swap
- Visible from the front — part of the aesthetic

**Tower light mount:**
- M18 Tower Light clamped to the top header with a pipe clamp
- Battery mounted behind the header board
- Runs all day on a single XC5.0

**Speaker mount:**
- M18 Bluetooth Speaker hung from a hook on the rear of a frame leg
- Plays carnival music / hype tracks from a phone

### 8. Optional: SNIPER PORT (Inflator)

One additional port at the very bottom center, smaller (1.5" diameter). The M18 Compact Inflator with its precision nozzle can be inserted here for a focused, narrow air jet aimed straight up through the scoring zone.

This is the "skill shot" — less power but more precision than the blower. Could be a bonus tool earned for hitting a certain score, or always available as a strategic choice.

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Frame & Board (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut ¾" plywood to 48×72"
2. Cut 1×6 edge rails — two at 72" (sides), two at 48" (top/bottom)
3. Attach side rails to plywood with screws
4. Drill peg holes in plywood — offset grid, 5" H × 4" V spacing, rows starting at 12" and ending at 54" from bottom
5. Insert and glue ¾" dowel pegs (4" long)
6. Build and attach A-frame rear legs with hinges
7. Install cross brace, chain angle limiters, rubber feet
8. Mount side handles
9. Paint back panel matte black
10. Paint edge rails Milwaukee Red

### Phase 2: Air Ports (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Mark air port positions on both side rails: 8", 28", 48" from bottom
2. Drill 3" holes through side rails using hole saw
3. Sand or ream holes smooth
4. Print or fabricate 6 funnel inserts
5. Install rubber gaskets in funnels
6. Mount funnels into port holes with screws
7. Test-fit M18 blower nozzle in each port — should seat snugly with gasket contact
8. Verify airflow exits the board interior by holding tissue paper inside field

### Phase 3: Scoring & Ball Return (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Cut ¼" plywood strips for pocket dividers (4" tall × 5" deep)
2. Mark divider positions on bottom interior of board
3. Attach dividers with screws + wood glue
4. Paint pocket zones to color scheme (black gutters, white/yellow/orange/red scoring)
5. Drill 2" drainage holes behind each pocket
6. Build rear collection chute from 1×4 lumber — V-shape, full width, angled toward center
7. Mount chute to back of board
8. Install center divider in chute
9. Place collection bins
10. Test ball flow: drop from top → pegs → pocket → drainage → chute → bin

### Phase 4: Drop System & Finishing (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Cut 3 drop slots in top rail (3" wide, at 12"/24"/36" from left)
2. Fabricate ball guides (bent aluminum or 3D-print curved ramps)
3. Mount ball holder tray to front of top rail
4. Build top header from 1×8, paint black, add "CYCLONE PLINKO" lettering
5. Mount header to top of board
6. Build 2 step stools from scrap 2×4 (12" high, 16"×16" platform)

### Phase 5: Acrylic & Final Assembly (Weekend 2, Day 2 continued)
1. Drill acrylic to match all peg holes
2. Mount acrylic to edge rails with wing nuts (removable for maintenance)
3. Seal all edges — verify no ball escape routes
4. Install tether eyebolts in frame legs
5. Attach bungee tethers with carabiners
6. Clip M18 blowers to tethers
7. Mount M18 Tower Light to header with pipe clamp
8. Hang M18 Speaker on rear frame hook
9. Mount battery station on cross brace
10. Load batteries, power everything on
11. FULL PLAYTEST — drop balls, blast from all ports, verify scoring, verify ball return

---

## Setup on Game Day

1. Unfold legs, secure angle chains (2 min)
2. Set board in position, verify stability on ground
3. Clip M18 blowers to tethers (30 sec)
4. Insert batteries into blowers, tower light, speaker (1 min)
5. Turn on tower light and speaker
6. Load ball holder trays with whiffle balls
7. **Play**

**Teardown:** Reverse. Remove batteries, unclip blowers, fold legs, carry by handles. Two people, five minutes.
