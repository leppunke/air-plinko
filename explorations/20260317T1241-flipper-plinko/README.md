# Flipper Plinko — Snap-Together Pinball Plinko

## The Problem This Solves
The OG Air Plinko is a beast: 80+ lbs of plywood, acrylic, and lumber. It needs a truck, two strong adults, and an hour of assembly. Stomp Rockets went even bigger (7ft board, stomp pads, PVC plumbing). These are awesome games trapped in inconvenient packages.

**Flipper Plinko asks: what if the fabrication IS the feature?**

## Concept
A pinball/plinko hybrid where kids launch balls up into a peg field, watch them cascade down, then SAVE them with pinball flippers at the bottom to send them back up for another run. Every ball gets multiple lives — the longer you keep it in play, the more points you rack up.

The entire game is built on **standard hardware-store pegboard**. Every peg, every scoring bucket, every flipper bracket hooks or snaps onto the pegboard. The frame is PVC conduit with 3D-printed snap connectors. The front panel slides into channel rails. **Zero tools. Ten-minute setup. Fits in the trunk of a Honda Civic.**

## How It Plays

### Setup
Two players stand side-by-side in front of a 4ft × 5ft tilted pegboard. Each player has:
- A spring-loaded plunger (bottom corner) to launch balls
- A flipper paddle (bottom-center of their half) controlled by a squeeze handle

### Gameplay (60-second rounds)
1. **LAUNCH** — Pull the plunger, send a whiffle ball arcing up into the peg field
2. **WATCH** — Ball cascades through a forest of neon snap-in pegs, bouncing unpredictably
3. **SCORE** — Ball lands in a scoring bucket (hooked onto the pegboard at various heights): 10 / 25 / 50 / 100 pts
4. **OR SAVE IT** — If the ball drops to the bottom without scoring, it rolls toward your flipper. Squeeze the handle → flipper swings up → ball launches back into the peg field for another cascade
5. **RACK UP** — Each time a ball passes through the peg field it scores wherever it lands. A single ball can score 3-4 times if you keep flipping it back
6. **DRAIN** — Miss the flipper and the ball drops into the gutter. Gone. Next ball.

Each player gets 5 balls. Round ends at 60 seconds or when all balls are drained.

### Scoring
- Balls accumulate points across multiple passes
- Buckets: Gutter (0) → Bottom shelf (10) → Mid sides (25) → Upper wings (50) → Top center jackpot (100)
- **Combo bonus**: If a ball scores on 3+ passes without draining, its final score is doubled

### The Flipper Mechanic
The flippers are the magic. They're 3D-printed paddles mounted on a pivot bolt, spring-loaded with a rubber band, and actuated by a bicycle brake lever mounted on the side rail. Squeeze the lever → cable pulls → flipper swings up 60°. Release → rubber band snaps it back down. Simple, mechanical, satisfying.

The flipper doesn't guarantee a save — you have to time it. The ball rolls down an angled trough toward the flipper, and if you squeeze too early or too late, it slips past. This creates a **skill loop**: launch → watch → anticipate → flip → celebrate or groan.

## The Fabrication Revolution

### What Goes In The Car
Two bundles, one bag:

| Package | Contents | Size | Weight |
|---------|----------|------|--------|
| **Panel Bundle** | Pegboard back + polycarbonate front, rubber-banded together | 48" × 60" × 1" | ~18 lbs |
| **Frame Kit** (duffel bag) | PVC pipes, 3D-printed connectors, pegs, buckets, flippers, plungers, hardware | 36" × 12" × 8" | ~12 lbs |

**Total: ~30 lbs. Fits in any vehicle with fold-down seats.**

### Assembly (10 minutes, zero tools)
1. **Snap frame** (2 min) — Push PVC pipes into 3D-printed corner joints. Click. Frame stands up.
2. **Hang pegboard** (1 min) — Pegboard drops into channel rails on the frame. Gravity holds it.
3. **Pop in pegs** (3 min) — Push 3D-printed pegs into pegboard holes. Standard pegboard hook spacing.
4. **Hook on buckets** (1 min) — Scoring buckets hook onto pegboard like pegboard bins at the hardware store.
5. **Clip on flippers** (1 min) — Flipper assemblies bolt onto the bottom rail with wing nuts (the ONE fastener).
6. **Slide in front panel** (1 min) — Polycarbonate sheet slides into channel rails from the top. Done.
7. **Attach plungers & ball trough** (1 min) — Snap into place at the bottom.

### Why Pegboard?
- **$12 at Home Depot** for a 4×8 sheet (cut to 4×5)
- **Lightweight** — a 4×5 pegboard weighs ~8 lbs vs ~35 lbs for ¾" plywood
- **Pre-drilled** — holes on 1" centers, perfect for snap-in pegs
- **Reconfigurable** — Move pegs between rounds! Easy mode for kindergartners, chaos mode for 5th graders
- **Replaceable** — If it gets damaged, $12 and you're back in business

### Why PVC + 3D Printed Connectors?
- **¾" PVC conduit is $3 for 10 ft** and cuts with a hacksaw
- **3D-printed snap joints** mean no glue, no screws — press-fit connections
- **Modular** — frame breaks into 6 pipes + 8 connectors that fit in a duffel
- **Rigid enough** for a carnival game, light enough for one person to carry

## Vibe
**"Arcade night at the hardware store."** The whole game looks like it was born in the pegboard aisle — because it was. Neon-colored pegs popping out of raw pegboard. PVC pipe frame. Clear front panel. It's utilitarian and fun, like a go-kart made from lawnmower parts.

The flipper mechanic adds the "one more try" energy of pinball. Kids don't just watch their ball fall — they ENGAGE with it. The squeeze-and-flip creates a physical connection to every ball's fate. The crowd gasps when a ball barely catches the flipper edge and arcs back up into the 100-point zone.

**The dads who built it carry it in from the parking lot in one trip, set it up while the other booth is still unpacking, and spend the rest of the day watching kids lose their minds.**
