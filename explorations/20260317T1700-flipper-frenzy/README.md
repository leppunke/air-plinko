# FLIPPER FRENZY — The Portable Pinball Riot

## The Origin Story
Flipper Plinko (8.1/10) was the most practical game ever designed for a school carnival — and the most forgettable. It fit in a Honda Civic, cost $200, and assembled in 10 minutes. It also scored the lowest memorability (7/10) and lowest chaos (6/10) of any concept. Kids would play it, shrug, and move on.

**Flipper Frenzy asks: what if we kept everything that made Flipper Plinko genius and fixed everything that made it boring?**

## What Changed

| | Flipper Plinko | Flipper Frenzy |
|---|---|---|
| Balls in play | 1 at a time, 5 per round | Up to 6 simultaneously, auto-fed |
| Flippers per player | 1 | 2 (left hand + right hand) |
| Peg types | Static mushroom caps | Static + SPINNER pegs + CHIME pegs |
| Player interaction | Parallel (separate sides) | Shared — THE WARP ZONE connects both sides |
| Sound | Silent | Aluminum chime pegs create cascading melodies |
| Chaos level | 6/10 "controlled, rhythmic" | 9/10 "barely contained riot" |
| Weight | ~30 lbs | ~33 lbs |
| Setup time | 10 min | 10 min |
| Fits in a sedan | Yes | Yes |

## Concept
A two-player pinball/plinko hybrid where balls auto-feed into a neon peg field every 5 seconds, cascade through a forest of static pegs, SPINNER pegs (that redirect the next ball), and CHIME pegs (that sing), and players frantically work TWO flippers each (left and right hand) to save balls from draining. Balls that hit the center WARP ZONE tunnel cross to the other player's side — your clutch save might accidentally score for your opponent.

The entire game is still built on **standard hardware-store pegboard**. Every component snaps, hooks, or press-fits onto the board. The frame is PVC with 3D-printed connectors. **Zero tools. Ten-minute setup. Fits in a sedan. Weighs 33 lbs.** But now the board MOVES, SINGS, and creates absolute pandemonium.

## How It Plays

### Setup
Two players stand side-by-side in front of a 4ft x 5ft tilted pegboard. Each player has:
- **Two flipper paddles** — left hand and right hand, controlled by squeeze handles on each side rail
- **A ball magazine** on top that auto-feeds a new ball every 5 seconds via gravity gate

### Gameplay (60-second rounds)
1. **AUTO-FEED** — A ball drops from the top magazine every 5 seconds. No launching needed. Balls just keep coming.
2. **CASCADE** — Ball bounces through the peg field. Static pegs bounce it randomly. **Spinner pegs** (3D-printed windmills on bearings) get spun by the impact and redirect the NEXT ball that hits them. The board is constantly reconfiguring itself.
3. **CHIME** — Some pegs have aluminum tube chimes behind them. As balls cascade, they create a unique descending melody every run. The board literally sings. No two runs sound the same.
4. **SCORE** — Ball lands in a scoring bucket (10 / 25 / 50 / 100 pts) OR...
5. **WARP** — Ball enters the WARP ZONE (a tunnel through the center divider) and crosses to the OTHER player's peg field. Your saved ball cascades through THEIR pegs. Could land in their jackpot. Could drain on their side. Pure chaos.
6. **SAVE IT** — Ball drops toward the drain. Left flipper or right flipper? Pick the right one. Squeeze. THWACK. Ball arcs back up into the peg field for another run. With up to 6 balls cascading simultaneously, you're flipping CONSTANTLY.
7. **DRAIN** — Miss both flippers. Ball gone. But another one drops in 5 seconds. The chaos never stops.

Round ends at 60 seconds. Timer bell rings. Remaining balls drain. Count your buckets.

### The Spinner Pegs
Every 4th peg is a **spinner** — a 3D-printed four-armed windmill mounted on a miniature skateboard bearing, press-fit into a pegboard hole. When a ball hits a spinner arm, the spinner rotates, and the NEXT ball that passes gets redirected at a completely different angle. The board state is constantly changing based on what happened before. Two identical launches produce wildly different cascades because the spinners remember the last impact.

**20 spinners** scattered through the field means the chaos multiplies with every ball. By mid-round, with 4-5 balls cascading and spinners whirling from recent hits, the peg field is a living, shifting maze.

### The Chime Pegs
**12 pegs** have a 4" aluminum tube (cut from wind chime stock) mounted behind the pegboard, positioned so the ball strikes the tube as it passes the peg. Each tube is a different length = different pitch. The tubes are arranged in a rough descending scale from top to bottom.

The result: every ball cascade plays a unique, semi-random melody as it bounces down through the chime pegs. Multiple balls create overlapping harmonies. The board has a SOUNDTRACK and it changes every second. Parents hear it from two booths away and walk over to investigate.

### The Warp Zone
A 3.5" diameter tunnel through the center divider at the midpoint of the board. Any ball that hits it at the right angle (roughly 1 in 5 balls that reach the center) passes through to the other player's peg field.

This creates:
- **Offense**: A lucky warp could score in your opponent's high-value bucket — but it counts for THEM
- **Defense**: Their warped ball could drain on your side — wasting one of their cascade opportunities
- **Chaos**: Neither player fully controls where balls end up. The center of the board is a no-man's-land
- **Stories**: "My ball warped through and landed in their JACKPOT. I literally scored for them." That's the sentence that makes this game legendary

### The Dual Flippers
Each player controls TWO flippers — left hand squeeze handle operates the left flipper, right hand operates the right. The flippers are offset (not symmetrical) — left flipper covers the left drain lane, right flipper covers the right drain lane, with a small gap in the center (the "death gap") that neither flipper reaches.

With 6 balls in play simultaneously:
- You're watching multiple balls cascade at once
- You're deciding LEFT or RIGHT for each one
- Sometimes two balls reach your flippers at the same time — you save one, lose one
- The dual-flipper mechanic turns passive watching into frantic two-handed action
- Kids who master both hands simultaneously develop a flow state

## The Portability Promise (Still Kept)

### What Goes In The Car
| Package | Contents | Size | Weight |
|---------|----------|------|--------|
| **Panel Bundle** | Pegboard + polycarbonate, rubber-banded | 48" x 60" x 1" | ~18 lbs |
| **Frame Kit** (duffel bag) | PVC pipes, connectors, pegs, spinners, chimes, buckets, flippers, hardware | 36" x 14" x 10" | ~15 lbs |

**Total: ~33 lbs. Still fits in any sedan with fold-down seats.** Only 3 lbs heavier than original Flipper Plinko — the spinners' bearings and the chime tubes are the only additions.

### Assembly (10 minutes, zero tools)
Same snap-together process as Flipper Plinko. The spinners press into pegboard holes just like regular pegs. The chime tubes hook on the back of the pegboard with printed clips. The second set of flippers adds 1 minute to setup (2 more wing nuts). The ball magazine hooks onto the top crossbar.

## Vibe
**"Arcade riot at the hardware store."** The original Flipper Plinko was a quiet, controlled experience. Flipper Frenzy is what happens when you take that same $200 hardware-store-pegboard DNA and inject it with multi-ball madness, spinning chaos, musical cascades, interdimensional warp tunnels, and two-fisted flipper panic.

The board MOVES (spinners whirling from impacts). The board SINGS (chime pegs creating random melodies). The board CONNECTS (warp zone sending balls between players). And the players are in a constant state of controlled frenzy — eyes darting between multiple cascading balls, hands squeezing left-right-left-right, screaming when a ball warps through to the other side.

It still fits in a Honda Civic. It still costs ~$220. It still assembles in 10 minutes with zero tools. But now it's the game kids talk about on the car ride home.

**"Remember that game where the pegs were SPINNING and it made MUSIC and my ball went through a PORTAL to the other side?!"**

That's the sentence. That's a 10/10 memorability. That's Flipper Frenzy.
