# Popsicle Plinko — Build Plan

## Concept
A 4×5 ft plinko board styled to look like giant craft supplies. Clothespin-snap launchers at top, pushpin-styled pegs, rubber band bumper rows, spinning pinwheels, and craft-cup scoring funnels at bottom. Every player takes home a mini craft kit to build their own shoebox version.

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Width | 48" (4 ft) | Two launchers side by side |
| Height | 60" (5 ft) | Playable for kids and adults standing |
| Depth (gap) | 4" | 1.5" wooden ball + clearance |
| Tilt angle | ~20° from vertical | Gravity drives the cascade |
| Frame footprint | 48"W × 30"D × 64"H | Including easel legs |

## Architecture

### 1. Frame & Playing Field
- **Back panel:** 4×5 ft sheet of ¾" plywood, painted kraft-brown (construction paper look) with a subtle grid pattern in lighter brown
- **Front panel:** 4×5 ft clear acrylic sheet (¼" thick), mounted with standoffs
- **Edge rails:** 1×4 lumber on all four sides, painted to look like giant popsicle sticks — rounded ends, light wood color with visible "grain" lines painted on. These are the signature visual element
- **Gap spacers:** 4" wooden blocks between back panel and acrylic at corners and mid-edges
- **Popsicle stick trim:** Additional 1×4 pieces with rounded ends added as decorative cross-braces on the frame exterior — reinforces the craft aesthetic

### 2. Support Frame
- **Rear legs (×2):** 2×4 lumber, 60" long, hinged at the top of the back panel for fold-flat transport
- **Cross brace:** Horizontal 2×4 between rear legs at 24" height
- **Angle limiter:** Chain between legs and back panel sets 20° tilt
- **Base feet:** Rubber caps on 2×4 feet
- **Handles:** Rope handles through holes in the edge rails, wrapped in colorful duct tape
- **Fasteners:** Wing nuts + carriage bolts at all breakdown joints
- **Transport:** Folds flat to ~6" thick. Two-person carry with rope handles

### 3. Pushpin Pegs
- **Material:** ¾" wooden dowels, 3.5" long (protruding into the 4" gap)
- **Pushpin caps:** 3D-printed discs (1.5" diameter, ½" thick) with a slight dome, painted in bright primary colors (red, yellow, blue, green). Press-fit or glued onto the front end of each dowel
- **Layout:** Offset plinko grid, 6" horizontal spacing, 5" vertical spacing
- **Count:** ~80 pegs total across the field
- **Mounting:** Drilled through back panel, glued in place. Matching holes drilled in acrylic for alignment pins (pegs don't pass through acrylic — they float in the gap)

### 4. Rubber Band Bumper Zones (×3 rows)
- **Positions:** At 25%, 50%, and 75% of board height
- **Construction:** Pairs of sturdy ¾" dowel posts (2" tall above back panel) spaced 8" apart, with a thick rubber band (or elastic cord) stretched between them
- **Per row:** 5 bumper pairs spanning the width, staggered left-right between rows
- **Total:** 15 rubber band bumpers across 3 rows
- **Colors:** Row 1 (top): Red bands. Row 2 (mid): Yellow bands. Row 3 (bottom): Blue bands
- **Effect:** Ball hits the stretched band and gets an unpredictable bounce. Bands vibrate visibly and make a satisfying twang sound
- **Replacement:** Bands wear out — keep a bag of spares. 30-second swap

### 5. Spinning Pinwheels (×2)
- **Position:** Mid-board, one in each player's half, offset vertically
- **Construction:** 3D-printed pinwheel (6" diameter, 4 blades) on a low-friction bolt axle through the back panel
- **Design:** Spiral lollipop pattern — alternating bright colors per blade
- **Bearing:** Nylon washer + lock nut, loose enough to spin freely when struck
- **Effect:** Ball hits a blade, pinwheel spins, ball deflects randomly. Visual spectacle — the spinning wheel catches the eye

### 6. Clothespin Launchers (×2)
- **Position:** Top of the board, left and right sides
- **Mechanism:** Spring-loaded lever arm (like a giant clothespin jaw)
  - Base: 2×4 block mounted to the top edge rail
  - Arm: 1×3 piece, 10" long, pivoting on a bolt at one end
  - Spring: Screen door spring connecting arm to base — pulls arm closed
  - Ball cup: Small 3D-printed cradle on the jaw end, holds the 1.5" ball
  - Stop pin: Limits arm travel to prevent over-snap
- **Operation:** Kid places ball in the cup, pulls the arm back against the spring (3-4" of travel), releases — SNAP! Ball launches into the peg field at an angle
- **Feed:** Balls sit in a gravity tray above the launcher. After each snap, the next ball rolls into the cup
- **Gravity tray:** Angled 1×4 channel with a lip, holds 5 balls in a row. Rear ball feeds forward as front ball is launched
- **Aesthetic:** Painted to look like a giant wooden clothespin — tapered jaw shape, spring visible and exaggerated
- **Safety:** Low spring tension — enough to launch a 1.5" wooden ball ~12" into the field, not enough to hurt fingers. Arm has a rubberized grip area

### 7. Scoring Funnels (×7)
- **Position:** Bottom of the board, spanning the full width
- **Construction:** Funnels made from 4" PVC pipe segments (3" tall), painted to look like upside-down craft paint cups — each a different color matching its score
- **Funnel tops:** 3D-printed flared rings that guide balls into the cup opening
- **Behind-board routing:** Each funnel connects to a tube that routes the ball behind the board to a common collection tray
- **Labels:** Score values painted on the front of each cup in bold craft-style lettering

| Position | Funnel Color | Points | Opening Width |
|----------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Center | Gold (glitter paint) | 100 | 3" |
| Center ±1 | Silver (metallic) | 50 | 4" |
| Center ±2 | Blue | 25 | 5" |
| Outer edges | Red | 10 | 6" |

### 8. Ball Return
- **Collection tray:** Angled channel behind the board at the bottom
- **Return tubes:** Two PVC tubes (one per player) routing balls from the collection tray back up to each player's gravity tray
- **Manual reset:** Between rounds, an operator lifts balls from the collection tray and reloads the gravity trays. Simple and reliable — no complex auto-return needed
- **Ball count:** 10 painted wooden balls total (5 per player), plus 5 spares

### 9. Booth Dressing
- **Sign:** "POPSICLE PLINKO" in giant craft-style bubble letters across the top. Letters look hand-cut from construction paper (actually painted plywood cutouts)
- **Side panels:** Foam board panels painted with craft supply imagery — scissors, glue bottles, crayons — frames the booth
- **Scoreboard:** Chalkboard-painted panel on the side. Operator writes scores with chalk
- **Craft kit display:** A sample completed mini plinko board on display next to the booth, so kids can see what they're taking home
- **Kit distribution:** Ziplock bags in a bin, handed out after each round

### 10. Take-Home Craft Kit Station
- **Pre-assembled kits:** Ziplock bags containing:
  - 10 jumbo popsicle sticks
  - 3 thick rubber bands
  - 6 flat-head thumbtacks (in a small sealed bag, labeled "grown-up helper needed")
  - 2 glass marbles
  - 1 wooden clothespin
  - 1 double-sided instruction card (card stock, laminated)
- **Kit cost:** ~$1.50 per kit in bulk
- **Quantity:** Prep 200 kits for a full day carnival
- **Kit prep day:** 1 hour with 2-3 helpers to assemble 200 kits

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Frame & Board (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut plywood to 48×60"
2. Round the ends of 1×4 edge rails with a jigsaw (popsicle stick shape)
3. Attach edge rails to plywood edges with screws
4. Sand and round all exposed edges
5. Paint back panel kraft-brown with light grid lines
6. Paint edge rails light wood color with painted grain lines
7. Build rear legs with hinges, chain angle limiter
8. Mount rope handles through edge rails
9. Label breakdown joints

### Phase 2: Pegs & Obstacles (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Mark peg grid on back panel — 6" horizontal, 5" vertical, offset rows
2. Drill 80 peg holes
3. Cut 80 dowels to 3.5"
4. Glue pushpin caps (3D printed) onto dowel ends
5. Insert and glue all pegs
6. Install rubber band bumper posts (30 post pairs across 3 rows)
7. Stretch rubber bands between post pairs — test tension
8. Mount pinwheel axle bolts, install pinwheels — test spin freely
9. Test ball clearance through peg field without acrylic

### Phase 3: Launchers & Scoring (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Build clothespin launcher mechanisms (×2)
   - Mount base blocks to top edge rail
   - Attach lever arms with pivot bolts
   - Install screen door springs
   - Attach ball cradles (3D printed)
   - Install stop pins
   - Build gravity feed trays above launchers
2. Build and paint 7 scoring funnels from PVC
3. Attach 3D-printed flare rings to funnels
4. Mount funnels along bottom edge
5. Build ball collection tray behind board
6. Test: launch → cascade → score → collect. Iterate

### Phase 4: Seal, Finish & Kits (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Paint scoring funnels (gold, silver, blue, red)
2. Paint score values on funnels
3. Drill acrylic for spacer alignment pins
4. Mount acrylic front panel with spacer blocks
5. Seal all edges — no ball escape routes
6. Build and paint booth sign — "POPSICLE PLINKO" bubble letters
7. Paint chalkboard scoreboard panel
8. Build craft kit display (sample completed mini board)
9. Final play testing — full rounds with all 10 balls
10. Assemble 200 craft kits (1 hour, 2-3 helpers)
