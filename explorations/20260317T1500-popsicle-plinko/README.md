# POPSICLE PLINKO

## Concept
A carnival-scale plinko board designed to look like it was built from giant craft supplies — oversized popsicle sticks as structural beams, huge pushpin-style pegs, rubber band bumper zones, clothespin-snap launchers, and scoring funnels made from painted craft cups. The whole thing screams "a kid built this" even though it's 5 feet tall and built from plywood by two dads with Milwaukee tools.

The secret weapon: every kid who plays walks away with a **Take-Home Craft Kit** — a ziplock bag with popsicle sticks, rubber bands, a marble, and a one-page instruction card to build a working mini plinko board on a shoebox lid. The carnival game IS the craft project, just supersized. Kids go home, build the mini version, and relive the carnival for weeks.

## The Pitch
*"Step right up to POPSICLE PLINKO! Pull back the giant clothespin, load your marble, and SNAP! Watch it bounce through rubber bands and pushpin pegs — can you hit the GOLDEN CUP? Every player takes home a kit to build their own!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- 4×5 ft plinko board tilted at ~20° from vertical, mounted on a craft-themed frame
- Board face is painted to look like a giant piece of construction paper (kraft brown or bright green)
- Two clothespin launchers at the top — one per player
- 5 painted wooden balls (marbles at carnival scale — 1.5" diameter) per player
- Clear acrylic front panel so spectators watch the cascade

### Mechanics
- **CLOTHESPIN LAUNCHERS:** Giant spring-loaded clothespin mechanisms at the top of the board. Kid places a ball in the jaw, pulls back the top lever against a spring, and SNAP — ball launches into the peg field. Satisfying click + spring sound. Different pull-back distances = different launch angles into the field
- **PUSHPIN PEGS:** Wooden dowel pegs painted to look like giant pushpins (colored thumbtack heads on top). Classic plinko offset grid layout. 6" spacing. Ball bounces left-right down through the field
- **RUBBER BAND BUMPER ZONES:** Three horizontal rows of thick rubber bands stretched across the board between posts. Balls that hit a rubber band get an unpredictable bounce — sometimes straight up, sometimes sideways. These are the chaos multipliers. Brightly colored bands (red, yellow, blue) that vibrate visibly when struck
- **SPINNING PINWHEELS:** Two 3D-printed pinwheel obstacles mid-board. When a ball hits a pinwheel blade, the pinwheel spins (satisfying visual) and deflects the ball in a random direction. The pinwheels have craft-style designs (spiral lollipop pattern)
- **SCORING FUNNELS:** 7 funnels at the bottom, made from (or painted to look like) upside-down craft paint cups. Each funnel catches balls and routes them to a labeled scoring zone

### Scoring
| Zone | Points | Width | Look |
|------|--------|-------|------|
| GOLDEN CUP (center) | 100 | 3" | Gold glitter paint cup |
| SILVER CUPS (×2) | 50 | 4" | Silver metallic cups |

### Win Condition
- Each player launches 5 balls
- **Competitive:** Highest total score wins a prize upgrade
- **Craft bonus:** Every player wins the take-home craft kit regardless of score
- **Jackpot:** Hit the Golden Cup and ring the bell — winner gets a special golden marble for their home kit

## The Take-Home Craft Kit

This is what makes Popsicle Plinko different. Every kid walks away with the game.

### Kit Contents (est. $1.50 per kit)
- 10 jumbo popsicle sticks
- 3 thick rubber bands
- 6 pushpins (for pegs, with parent help)
- 2 glass marbles
- 1 mini wooden clothespin (launcher)
- 1 instruction card (double-sided, illustrated)

### Home Build Instructions (on the card)
1. Grab a shoebox lid (or cut a cereal box panel)
2. Glue popsicle sticks as a border/frame around the edge
3. Push pushpins through the cardboard in a zigzag peg pattern
4. Stretch rubber bands across the board between pins for bumper zones
5. Cut small cups from an egg carton and tape to the bottom as scoring funnels
6. Prop the board up at an angle (lean on a book)
7. Use the clothespin as a launcher at the top — pinch, load marble, release!
8. **PLAY!**

Build time: 15-20 minutes with a parent. Replayable forever.

## What Makes It Special
1. **THE CRAFT KIT** — No other carnival game sends you home with the game itself. Kids build it that afternoon. Parents love it. Teachers love it. It's the gift that keeps giving
2. **Aesthetic magic** — The giant craft-supply look is instantly recognizable and delightful. Adults smile at the cleverness. Kids think it's hilarious that pushpins are 4 inches tall
3. **Clothespin snap** — The launcher mechanic is viscerally satisfying and immediately understood by every kid. Pull back, let go. No instructions needed
4. **Rubber band chaos** — Visible, jiggly, unpredictable. The bands vibrate and twang when hit. Kids can SEE the chaos happening
5. **Pinwheel eye candy** — Spinning pinwheels are mesmerizing. Every hit creates a visual reward even if the score isn't great
6. **Self-reinforcing loop** — Play the big version → take home the kit → build the small version → remember the carnival → tell friends → they want to play next year
7. **Accessible construction** — Two dads, two weekends, Home Depot lumber, spray paint. The "craft supply" aesthetic means imperfect cuts and wobbly paint actually HELP the look
8. **Line entertainment** — Kids in line can peek at the instruction card from their kit bag and get hyped to build their own version. The anticipation IS the entertainment

## Vibe
Think: Art class meets Plinko meets that feeling of opening a craft kit on a rainy Saturday. The whole booth looks like it fell out of a giant's art supply drawer. It's approachable, warm, creative, and chaotic. Kids don't just play a game — they join a maker moment.
