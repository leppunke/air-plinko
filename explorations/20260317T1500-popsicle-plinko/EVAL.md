## Composite Score: 8.6 / 10

## Strengths
1. **The take-home craft kit is the killer feature.** No other carnival game extends the experience beyond the booth. Kids build a working version at home, tell friends, create demand for next year's carnival. It's viral marketing disguised as a party favor
2. **Near-zero onboarding.** Clothespin snap is universally understood. No explanation needed. Line moves fast
3. **Construction simplicity.** Two dads, two weekends, basic tools. No electrical systems (unlike Air Plinko blower), no pneumatics (unlike Stomp Rockets). The "craft supply" aesthetic means rough cuts and wobbly paint IMPROVE the look
4. **Durability sweet spot.** Wood pegs + rubber bands + springs. Nothing fragile, nothing electronic, nothing expensive to replace
5. **The aesthetic sells itself.** Adults smile at the clever craft-supply theme. Kids are drawn to the bright colors. It photographs beautifully

## Weaknesses
1. **Lower physical intensity** compared to Stomp Rockets. Kids stand and pull a lever rather than jump and stomp. Less exhausting = less "oh my god that was wild" energy
2. **Sequential play** (5 balls per turn) rather than simultaneous chaos. Less spectacle per second than games with multi-ball launches
3. **4th-5th grade ceiling.** The craft aesthetic may feel "babyish" to older elementary kids. Could mitigate with a "speed round" mode or competitive scoring bracket
4. **Kit prep labor.** 200 craft kits at ~3 minutes each = 10 hours of assembly. Needs a volunteer kit-packing party. Material cost ($88) is modest but non-zero
5. **Pinwheel reliability.** Free-spinning pinwheels on bolt axles may stick over time with dust/grit. Need periodic lubrication or nylon washer replacement

## Verdict
Popsicle Plinko trades raw intensity for something more lasting: **every kid walks away with the game.** The take-home craft kit creates a memory loop — carnival → kit → home build → retelling → anticipation for next year — that no other concept achieves. Construction is the simplest of all concepts explored so far, and the craft aesthetic is inherently forgiving of imperfect builds.

**Best for:** Carnivals that want maximum long-term impact and a game that keeps giving after the event. Ideal when the goal is community engagement and creative play, not just adrenaline.

**Compared to Stomp Rockets Plinko (8.8):** Stomp Rockets wins on raw spectacle and physical intensity. Popsicle Plinko wins on memorability, ease of construction, and the unique take-home experience. If you can only build one: Stomp Rockets for "holy cow that was awesome." Popsicle Plinko for "remember that game? I built one at home!"
