# Tornado Alley — Build Plan

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Length | 60" (5 ft) | Two players face off at each end |
| Width | 36" (3 ft) | Wide enough for town layout |
| Height | 30" (2.5 ft) | Standard table height, works for kids standing |
| Lid gap | 6" | Clearance for buildings + ball flight |
| Total footprint | 60"L × 36"W × 36"H (with lid) |

---

## Architecture

### 1. Table Base
- **Tabletop:** 3/4" plywood, 60×36", painted matte black with white road grid lines ("Main Street" + cross streets)
- **Legs:** 4× collapsible banquet table legs (or 2×4 lumber with folding hinges) — 30" height
- **Edge walls:** 1×8 lumber on all four sides, forming a 6" deep playing arena
- **Corner gussets:** Plywood triangles at each corner for rigidity

### 2. Acrylic Lid
- **Material:** 1/4" clear acrylic sheet, 60×36"
- **Mounting:** Hinged on one long side (piano hinge) so it flips open for ball retrieval and building adjustment
- **Latch:** Two draw latches on the opposite long side to lock it down during play
- **Vortex ports:** 6 circular cutouts (3 per player side), 4" diameter, along each short end wall (NOT in the lid)
- **Port covers:** Simple rotating disc covers so unused ports stay closed

### 3. Town Buildings (Peg Obstacles)
All 3D printed or built from scrap wood blocks. Mounted to the tabletop with dowel pins (removable for reconfiguration).

| Building | Size (L×W×H) | Function |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Water Tower | 3×3×5" | Tall cylindrical deflector, center-ish. Balls ricochet wildly off the round shape |
| General Store | 5×3×3" | Rectangular blocker. Creates a wind shadow — balls hide behind it |
| Church + Steeple | 3×3×4" | Angled roof deflects air upward. Protects the 50pt shelter behind it |
| Houses (×4) | 2×2×2" | Small cube obstacles scattered around, create channels |
| Fences (×6) | 4×0.5×1.5" | Low walls that redirect ground-rolling balls. Printed with gaps so air passes through but balls don't |
| Trees (×4) | 1.5" dia × 3" | Cylindrical, mounted on bases. Random deflectors |

### 4. Storm Shelters (Scoring Pockets)
- **Construction:** Rectangular cutouts in the tabletop (3×4" openings) with a 3D-printed ramp leading down into a collection box underneath
- **One-way flap:** Thin flexible plastic flap at the entrance — ball pushes through, flap springs back. Air blasts can't eject a scored ball
- **Collection box:** Shallow tray under the table for each shelter, easily pulled out to retrieve balls
- **Color coding:** Each shelter entrance painted with its point value and color-matched to a flag on a small pole above it

| Shelter | Position | Opening Size | Flag Color |
|---------|----------|-------------|------------|
| Town Hall (100) | Dead center | 3" wide | Gold |
| Church (50) | Mid-field, offset left | 3.5" wide | Purple |
| School ×2 (25) | Mid-flanks, one per side | 4" wide | Blue |
| Barn ×2 (10) | Near corners, one per side | 5" wide | Green |

### 5. Vortex Cannons (×2 sets of 3)

Each player has access to 3 cannons, one per port.

**Per cannon:**
- **Body:** 5-gallon bucket (or 3-gallon for smaller hands), bottom cut out and replaced with a rubber exercise band membrane
- **Nozzle:** 3" diameter hole cut in the bucket lid (this aims into the port)
- **Mount:** Cannon sits in a cradle/holster attached to the outside of the table wall, nozzle aligned with the port
- **How it works:** Player pulls back on the rubber membrane (gripping a handle glued to the center), releases — THWUMP — a toroidal air vortex shoots through the port and across the field

**DIY Vortex Cannon Build:**
1. Cut bottom out of 5-gallon bucket
2. Stretch thick rubber band (exercise band) over the open bottom, secure with hose clamp
3. Glue a drawer pull to the center of the rubber membrane (grab handle)
4. Cut 3" hole in the bucket lid
5. Snap lid on, nozzle faces the table port

### 6. Timer System (Optional)
- **Simple option:** 60-second sand timer (giant novelty hourglass) mounted on a post at the center of one long side
- **Electric option:** Battery-powered countdown timer with a buzzer, start button on each player's side (both must press to start)
- **No-timer option:** Play until all balls are sheltered, fastest time wins

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Table (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut plywood tabletop to 60×36"
2. Cut and attach 1×8 edge walls on all four sides (screws + glue)
3. Add corner gussets for rigidity
4. Attach folding leg brackets and 2×4 legs
5. Cut 6 vortex ports (4" holes) in the short-side edge walls — 3 per side, evenly spaced
6. Make rotating port covers from scrap plywood

### Phase 2: Playing Field (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Paint tabletop matte black
2. Mask and paint white road grid (Main Street + cross streets)
3. Mark and cut storm shelter openings (6 rectangular cutouts)
4. Build and attach under-table collection trays for each shelter
5. Install one-way flaps on each shelter entrance
6. Paint shelter surrounds with point values and colors

### Phase 3: 3D Print & Town (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Print all buildings, fences, trees, shelter ramps, flags, cannon cradles
2. Paint buildings (classic small-town colors — red barn, white church, etc.)
3. Install dowel pin mounts in tabletop
4. Place buildings in the town layout, test ball flow and sightlines
5. Adjust building positions until the field feels chaotic but fair

### Phase 4: Cannons & Lid (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Build 6 vortex cannons (bucket + membrane + handle + nozzle)
2. Install cannon cradles on the outside of each port
3. Test each cannon — verify air vortex travels at least 3 feet across the field
4. Attach piano hinge and mount acrylic lid
5. Install draw latches
6. Full playtest: 6 balls, two players, 60 seconds. Tune building positions and port angles
