# TORNADO ALLEY

## Concept
A flat-table air hockey / plinko hybrid where two players wield **vortex cannons** to blast whiffle balls across a miniature town into storm shelters. The playing field is a horizontal tabletop covered in a clear acrylic lid, with 3D-printed buildings, trees, and fences acting as peg obstacles. Each player fires a handheld vortex cannon (built from a bucket + rubber membrane) through ports on their side of the table, creating real visible air blasts that scatter balls across "Main Street."

## The Pitch
*"A tornado just touched down on Main Street! You've got 60 seconds to blast every ball into a storm shelter before the other player's tornado sends them flying. Ready? PULL!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- 6 colored whiffle balls start in the center "town square"
- Each player stands at their end of the table
- A 60-second countdown timer starts when both players slam their start buttons

### Mechanics
- **Vortex cannons** fire through angled ports along each player's side — real air blasts that scatter lightweight balls
- Players choose which port to fire through (3 ports per side, different angles into the field)
- Between blasts, players must pull back the rubber membrane to "reload" — creates a satisfying ~1 second reload rhythm
- **Buildings** block and redirect airflow — balls hide behind the general store, ricochet off the water tower
- **Storm shelters** are recessed scoring pockets with one-way flap doors — balls roll in but can't be blasted back out

### Scoring
| Shelter | Points | Difficulty |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Town Hall (center) | 100 | Surrounded by buildings on all sides, tiny opening |
| Church (mid-field) | 50 | Protected by the steeple deflector |
| School (×2, flanks) | 25 | Wider openings but far from center |
| Barn (×2, near edges) | 10 | Big easy targets near the walls |

### Win Condition
- **Competitive mode:** Each player scores points for balls in shelters on their OPPONENT's half (you're blowing balls away from you)
- **Cooperative mode:** Combined score vs. target thresholds (Bronze: 100, Silver: 200, Gold: 300)

## What Makes It Special
1. **Real vortex physics** — kids physically feel the air blast, see balls scatter. No batteries, no electronics (except optional timer)
2. **The reload** — pulling back the membrane is deeply satisfying, like cocking a nerf gun. Creates rhythm and strategy
3. **Chaos cascades** — one good blast sends 3 balls careening off buildings into unexpected places
4. **Table-height play** — accessible to all ages, wheelchair-friendly, adults and kids play the same game
5. **The sound** — vortex cannons make a deep satisfying THWUMP that draws a crowd
6. **Visual spectacle** — balls pinballing off little buildings with every blast, pure mayhem on Main Street

## Vibe
Think: Rampage meets air hockey meets that satisfying feeling of knocking over dominos. Controlled destruction of a cute little town. Kids will be SCREAMING.
