# THE GREAT CRANK DERBY — Build Plan

## Concept
Head-to-head hand-crank racing game. Two players wind cranks to pull wooden race cars up a 7-foot vertical track. First car to hit the bell wins. Pure physical skill, zero luck.

## Architecture

### 1. Race Board (Main Structure)

- **Board:** Two sheets of ¾" plywood, joined to make 48" × 84" (or one sheet of ¾" plywood 4×8 cut to 48×84")
- **Track grooves:** Two parallel tracks routed into the plywood face, ¼" deep × 1" wide
- **Track path:** Each track follows the same mirrored S-curve pattern:
  - **Bottom section (0-24"):** Gentle 15° incline, mostly straight, slight curve outward
  - **Switchback section (24-48"):** Two hairpin turns, track doubles back on itself. Each switchback is ~8" tall with 4" radius turns
  - **Dead Man's Hill (48-66"):** Near-vertical straight climb, 18" of steep track
  - **Glory Run (66-84"):** Track curves back to a gentler 20° incline, sweeping inward toward center, ending at the bell
- **Track separation:** 20" center-to-center at the base, tracks weave but never closer than 6" to each other
- **Track paint:** Each track groove painted a different color — red track (left) and blue track (right) — with white lane lines on the edges for visibility

### 2. Pulley System

- **Pulleys per track:** 8 small pulleys (1" nylon sheave pulleys)
- **Pulley positions:** One at each track direction change (hairpin turn apex, curve entry/exit) plus one at the bottom entry and one at the top near the bell
- **Mounting:** Each pulley screwed into the plywood face with a small standoff bracket (¼" spacer) so the string clears the board surface
- **String:** Braided nylon cord, 3/32" diameter — strong, low stretch, low friction through pulleys
- **String path:** From crank spool → base pulley → follows track route through each pulley → attaches to car front eye bolt

### 3. Race Cars (×2)

- **Body:** Solid wood block, 4"L × 2"W × 1.5"H
- **Track interface:** ¼" deep × 1" wide groove routed into the bottom — rides on a raised rail within the track channel
- **Rail:** A ¼" × ½" hardwood strip (oak or maple) glued into the center of each track groove — the car's groove rides on this rail like a monorail
- **Front attachment:** Small eye bolt (#10) screwed into the front face, pull string tied to it
- **Weight:** ~4-6 oz — light enough to move easily but heavy enough to slide back reliably on reset
- **Finish:** Sanded smooth, painted red or blue, sealed with polyurethane for slick sliding
- **Decorative wheels:** 4 small 3D-printed wheel hubs glued to sides — non-functional, purely visual. 1" diameter, painted to match car color

### 4. Crank Stations (×2)

- **Structure:** Vertical 2×4 post (30" tall) mounted to a 2×4 base frame (24"W × 18"D)
- **Base frame:** Two 2×4 legs with a cross brace, weighted down with sandbags or bolted to the race board base
- **Crank handle:** 3D-printed crank arm (6" radius) with a comfortable grip handle, mounted on a ½" steel rod (axle) that passes through the 2×4 post
- **Spool:** 3D-printed spool, 3" diameter × 2" wide, mounted on the axle inside the post. String wraps around this spool
- **Bearing:** Two flanged bearings (½" bore) pressed into the 2×4 post on each side — smooth spinning with minimal friction
- **Ratchet mechanism:** 3D-printed ratchet gear on the spool + spring-loaded pawl mounted to the post. Allows string to wind but not unwind during play. Pawl has a release lever (small 3D-printed handle) that a volunteer pulls to disengage for reset
- **String exit:** String exits the spool housing through a small guide hole in the top of the post, runs up to the first pulley on the board

### 5. Bell & Victory Flag System

- **Bell:** Standard desk call bell (the kind you slap at a hotel front desk) mounted at the top of each track, positioned so the car physically pushes into the bell trigger as it reaches the finish line
- **Flag:** 3D-printed hinged flag arm (8" tall) with a fabric pennant. Held down by a spring-loaded catch. When the car pushes past the bell, a small lever releases the catch and the flag pops up via a torsion spring
- **Reset:** Volunteer pushes the flag back down until the catch clicks. 2-second operation.

### 6. Support Structure

- **Back legs:** Two 2×4 legs (84" tall) attached to the back of the board with hinges at the top — fold flat for transport
- **Angle brace:** Adjustable chain between each leg and the board back — sets the 5° lean angle
- **Base connection:** The crank station base frames bolt to the bottom of the race board with carriage bolts + wing nuts — creates one rigid unit
- **Cross brace:** Horizontal 2×4 between the back legs at 36" height
- **Anti-tip sandbags:** Two sandbags (25 lb each) on the back leg cross brace

### 7. Decorative Elements

- **Header sign:** 48" × 12" painted plywood sign across the top: "THE GREAT CRANK DERBY" in bold carnival lettering
- **Terrain zone labels:** Painted directly on the board next to each track section — "THE FLATS", "SWITCHBACKS", "DEAD MAN'S HILL", "GLORY RUN"
- **Checkered flag pattern:** Painted border around the top section (Glory Run zone)
- **Starting line:** Bold white line across both tracks at the bottom with "START" text
- **Racing stripes:** Diagonal red and blue stripes on the crank station posts

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Board & Tracks (Weekend 1, Day 1)

1. Cut plywood to 48" × 84"
2. Lay out track paths on the board with pencil — use a flexible curve ruler or bent PVC pipe as a guide
3. Route both track grooves: ¼" deep × 1" wide (use a plunge router with edge guide)
4. Glue hardwood rail strips into the center of each groove — this is the monorail the cars ride on
5. Sand track grooves smooth — cars must slide freely
6. Drill pulley mount holes at each track direction change (8 per track, 16 total)
7. Paint track grooves — red (left), blue (right) — mask off with painter's tape for clean lines

### Phase 2: Cars & Pulleys (Weekend 1, Day 2)

1. Cut car blanks from hardwood (4" × 2" × 1.5")
2. Route groove in car bottoms — must fit snugly on the rail with minimal wobble but no binding
3. Drill and install eye bolt in front of each car
4. Sand, paint, and seal cars
5. Glue on decorative 3D-printed wheel hubs
6. Install all 16 pulleys with standoff brackets
7. Test car sliding: place car on track, tilt board — car should slide smoothly from top to bottom under gravity

### Phase 3: Crank Stations (Weekend 2, Day 1)

1. Cut 2×4 crank posts to 30"
2. Build base frames (2×4 rectangles, 24" × 18")
3. Drill axle holes through posts, press in bearings
4. Assemble 3D-printed spools, ratchet gears, and pawls
5. Install axle through bearings, mount spool and crank arm
6. Test ratchet: crank should wind freely, string should not unwind when released
7. Install release lever — pulling it should disengage the pawl and allow free unwind
8. Mount string guide at top of each post

### Phase 4: Assembly & Finish (Weekend 2, Day 2)

1. Build back support legs, attach with hinges
2. Install chain angle limiters
3. Bolt crank stations to board base with wing nuts
4. Run nylon cord from each spool → through guide → up to first pulley → through all track pulleys → tie to car eye bolt
5. Adjust string length: car should be at START line when spool is fully unwound
6. Install bells at top of each track
7. Install flag mechanisms
8. Test full race: crank both cars to the top, verify bells ring, flags pop
9. Paint zone labels, starting line, header sign
10. Mount header sign
11. Test reset: pull release levers, cars slide to bottom, string unwinds cleanly
12. Place sandbags on back leg cross brace
13. Do 10 full test races — identify any binding, string tangling, or pulley issues
14. Final adjustments and touch-up paint

## Setup & Teardown (Game Day)

### Setup (~15 minutes)
1. Unfold back legs, connect chains
2. Bolt crank stations to board base (wing nuts, no tools)
3. Stand board upright, lean back onto legs
4. Place sandbags on cross brace
5. Check string routing — no tangles or crossed strings
6. Test both cranks — one full race
7. Ready

### Teardown (~10 minutes)
1. Remove sandbags
2. Unwind strings, remove cars, coil strings loosely
3. Unbolt crank stations (wing nuts)
4. Fold back legs flat against board
5. Board lays flat: 48" × 84" × ~3" thick. Fits in a truck bed or on a roof rack
6. Crank stations stack on top
