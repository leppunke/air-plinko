# THE GREAT CRANK DERBY

## Zero Plinko DNA. Pure Racing Fury.

Two players grab hand cranks and FURIOUSLY wind their wooden race cars up a 7-foot vertical track. String wraps around a spool, pulls the car up through S-curves and chicanes, and the first car to smack the bell at the top wins. No balls. No pegs. No scoring zones. Just raw kid energy converted directly into SPEED through gears, string, and pulleys.

## Why This Is Different

Every concept so far has been a plinko variant — drop a ball, watch it cascade, score in buckets. This game has:
- **No balls at all** — the game piece is a wooden car on a rail
- **No randomness** — pure skill, pure effort, pure speed
- **Physical exertion** — kids are CRANKING, sweating, screaming
- **Head-to-head racing** — side-by-side, elbow-to-elbow, visible progress
- **A finish line moment** — the bell ring is the climax, not a score tally
- **Crowd energy** — spectators can see both cars climbing in real time and cheer

## The Setup

A 4-foot-wide, 7-foot-tall plywood board stands upright with a slight backward lean (5° for stability). Two parallel tracks are routed into the face — shallow grooves that guide wooden race cars from bottom to top. The tracks aren't straight — they weave through S-curves, a switchback, and a steep final straightaway.

Each player stands at the base with a hand crank mounted on a waist-height platform. The crank winds string onto a spool. The string runs UP through a series of pulleys along the track, attached to the car. Crank faster = car goes faster. Simple as that.

But here's the thing: the tracks have **TERRAIN ZONES**.

### Terrain Zones (Bottom to Top)

1. **THE FLATS** (0-2 ft) — Smooth, gentle incline. Easy cranking to get rolling. Both cars move fast. Kids feel powerful.

2. **THE SWITCHBACKS** (2-4 ft) — Track doubles back on itself twice. The car still climbs but the string path goes through redirecting pulleys. Cranking feels harder because the mechanical advantage shifts — you're pulling more string per foot of climb. Kids have to sustain effort.

3. **DEAD MAN'S HILL** (4-5.5 ft) — Near-vertical section. This is where arms burn. The car barely crawls. Kids who went too hard in the flats start to fade. Smart racers who paced themselves pull ahead.

4. **THE GLORY RUN** (5.5-7 ft) — Final sprint to the bell. Track tips back to a gentler angle. The car FLIES. The crowd goes wild. First car to hit the spring-loaded bell at the top triggers a flag pop (spring-loaded hinged flag that flips up when the car hits a trigger at the top).

## The Cars

Wooden block cars (~4" long, 2" wide, 1.5" tall) with a groove on the bottom that rides the track rail. A small eye bolt on the front connects to the pull string. Cars are painted in team colors — red and blue. 3D-printed wheel hubs are decorative only (the car slides on the rail, not rolling on wheels — simpler and more reliable).

## The Crank Stations

Each player gets a crank handle mounted on a 2x4 post at ~30" height (belly-button level for a 2nd grader). The crank turns a 3" diameter spool that winds the pull string. The string runs from the spool, up to the first pulley at the base of the track, then follows the track route through guide pulleys at each turn.

A simple ratchet mechanism (3D-printed pawl on the spool) prevents the string from unwinding — so if a kid lets go of the crank, their car stays put instead of sliding back down.

## The Reset

After each race, a volunteer pulls a release lever on each spool (lifts the ratchet pawl), and both cars slide back to the start under gravity. The string unwinds cleanly. Takes about 5 seconds. Next two kids step up.

## The Experience

A line of 3rd graders. Two step up. They grab the cranks. Someone yells GO. Both kids CRANK like their lives depend on it. The cars shoot up the flats. The crowd watches the cars climb neck-and-neck through the switchbacks. One kid starts to pull ahead on Dead Man's Hill. The other kid digs deep, cranking harder. The cars hit the Glory Run — they're inches apart — the red car pulls ahead — DING! Flag pops. Red wins. The kid throws their arms up. The crowd erupts. Next two.

30-second games. 5-second resets. 100+ kids through in an hour. The line never stops moving.

## What Makes It a Core Memory

- They FELT it in their arms
- They raced someone they know
- The crowd was watching
- There was a BELL
- They either won gloriously or lost by inches
- They want to do it again immediately
