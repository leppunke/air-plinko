# MEGA PINBALL PLINKO

## Concept
A **GIANT real-life pinball machine** built from Home Depot lumber, 3D-printed parts, and hardware store springs. 4 feet wide, 6 feet tall, tilted at 20°. Two kids stand side-by-side, each controlling one massive mechanical flipper. A spring-loaded plunger launches whiffle balls into a plinko peg field where they cascade through spring-loaded PVC bumpers, loop-de-loop ramps, and spinning targets before landing in scoring lanes at the bottom. Clear acrylic front panel lets the whole crowd watch the chaos unfold.

Every kid knows pinball. Nobody has ever played one this big, made from real wood, where YOU are the left flipper and your friend is the right.

## The Pitch
*"Step up to the MEGA PINBALL! Grab a flipper — you're LEFT, you're RIGHT. Launch the ball and KEEP IT ALIVE! Hit the bumpers! Ride the ramp! Don't let it drain! Ready? PULL THE PLUNGER!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×6 ft playfield tilted at ~20° from vertical, mounted on a sturdy A-frame
- Two flipper control stations at the bottom — one LEFT flipper, one RIGHT flipper
- Spring-loaded plunger on the right side (classic pinball position)
- 5 whiffle balls per round, gravity-fed from a magazine above the plunger
- 90-second round timer (optional — or play until all 5 balls drain)

### Mechanics
- **FLIPPERS (×2):** Each flipper is a 14" wooden paddle on a heavy-duty pivot bolt, with a bungee cord return spring. The player pulls a lever/handle on the outside of the cabinet — a cable pulls the flipper up with a satisfying THWACK. Release and the bungee snaps it back down. One kid controls LEFT, one controls RIGHT. Forced teamwork (or hilarious miscommunication)
- **PLUNGER:** Classic pinball spring plunger mounted at bottom-right. Pull back and release — the spring shoots the ball up a launch lane (PVC half-pipe channel) into the top of the playfield. Stronger pull = faster launch
- **PEG FIELD:** Classic plinko-style offset rows of ¾" dowel pegs across the middle 60% of the playfield. Balls bounce chaotically between them. Pegs are painted in alternating neon colors
- **SPRING BUMPERS (×4):** 3D-printed or PVC cap mushroom bumpers mounted on compression springs bolted to the backboard. When a ball hits one, the spring compresses and LAUNCHES the ball in a random direction. Mounted in a diamond pattern in the upper playfield. Each bumper hit = 10 bonus points
- **LOOP RAMP (×1):** A bent PVC half-pipe ramp on the left side that curves up and loops the ball back to center-top of the playfield. Successfully riding the full ramp = 50 bonus points + the ball re-enters the peg field from above for more chaos
- **SPINNER TARGET (×1):** A 3D-printed spinning disc mounted center-top, with alternating colored wedges. Ball hits it, it spins wildly (visual spectacle). Each spin-through = 25 bonus points
- **DRAIN:** The gap between the two flippers at the bottom. If a ball passes through without being flipped back up, it's gone — rolls into the drain gutter and out the back. Lost ball
- **BALL SAVE GUTTER:** Narrow channels along the far left and far right edges — if a ball rolls down the outer wall, it feeds back to the flipper area for one more chance (like the inlane/outlane in real pinball). Only the CENTER drain is a true drain

### Scoring
| Target | Points | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| JACKPOT HOLE | 500 | Tiny pocket at top-center, behind the spinner. Ball must thread the needle |
| LOOP RAMP (complete) | 50 | Ball must ride the full ramp and exit at the top |
| SPRING BUMPER (each hit) | 10 | 4 bumpers, balls can hit the same one multiple times |
| SPINNER (each pass) | 25 | Center-top spinning disc |
| SCORING LANES (bottom) | 10-100 | 5 lanes between the flippers, center lane = 100, outer lanes = 10 |
| DRAIN | 0 | Ball lost forever. Sad trombone optional |

### Win Condition
- **5 balls per team.** Play until all 5 drain.
- **Competitive:** Teams of 2 rotate — beat the previous team's score
- **Cooperative:** Both players work together, aim for high score thresholds (Bronze: 200, Silver: 500, Gold: 1000)
- **Bonus:** If you clear all 5 balls through the JACKPOT HOLE, you win the mega prize

## What Makes It Special
1. **EVERYONE KNOWS PINBALL** — zero explanation needed. Kids see it and instantly know what to do. Pull plunger, flip flippers, keep ball alive. Onboarding: 5 seconds
2. **REAL MECHANICAL FLIPPERS** — not a button, not a screen. A physical lever that pulls a cable that swings a wooden paddle. Kids FEEL the flipper thwack through their hands. Deeply, viscerally satisfying
3. **FORCED TEAMWORK** — one kid per flipper. You MUST communicate. "YOURS! MINE! FLIP FLIP FLIP!" Creates instant bonding and hilarious failures. Best friends and strangers alike
4. **MULTI-SENSORY CHAOS** — balls pinging off spring bumpers (BOING), smacking into pegs (click-click-click), riding the ramp (swoooosh), spinning the spinner (whirrrr). The game has a living SOUNDTRACK of mechanical sounds
5. **VISIBLE FROM ACROSS THE CARNIVAL** — 6 feet tall, clear front panel, neon pegs, balls ricocheting everywhere. It's a spectacle. People will walk over just to watch
6. **SELF-RESETTING** — drained balls roll out the back, down a chute, into a collection bin. Reload the magazine, next team steps up. 30-second turnaround
7. **ENDLESSLY REPLAYABLE** — every ball takes a different path. No two games are the same. Kids will beg to go again
8. **DAD-FRIENDLY BUILD** — no electronics, no programming, no welding. Lumber, bolts, springs, PVC, and a drill. Two dads with Milwaukee M18 tools can build this over 2-3 weekends
