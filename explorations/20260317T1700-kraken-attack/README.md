# KRAKEN ATTACK

## Concept
A giant ring toss reimagined as a deep-sea battle. A 4×4 ft ocean scene rises from the carnival floor — a painted plywood seascape crawling with TENTACLES. Eight PVC tentacles of varying heights, thicknesses, and angles burst out of the surface at wild angles, each wrapped in pool noodle segments and painted in bioluminescent purples, greens, and pinks. Players stand 6ft back and hurl glow-in-the-dark rings at the tentacles. Land a ring on a tentacle, you've captured it. The taller and thinner the tentacle, the more points. Three rings on the same tentacle? KRAKEN CAPTURED — bonus jackpot.

This isn't your grandma's ring toss with sad little bottles. This is a MONSTER rising from the ocean floor, tentacles thrashing in every direction, and you're the hero trying to lasso them all.

## The Pitch
*"The Kraken is rising! Grab your rings and capture as many tentacles as you can! The tall skinny ones are worth the most — if you can land one. Three rings on the same tentacle? You've captured the KRAKEN! Ready? THROW!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Ocean platform: 4ft × 4ft plywood base, raised 12" on legs, painted deep ocean blue/black with waves, fish, and seaweed
- 8 tentacles: PVC pipes (varying ½"–2" diameter) rising 12"–36" from the surface at various angles (not all vertical — some lean, some curve)
- Glow rings: 12 rings per player (10" diameter, made from glow-stick necklaces zip-tied into loops, or 3D-printed rings with glow paint)
- Throwing line: 6ft from front edge of platform (adjustable: 4ft for K–1st)
- Optional: UV blacklight strip under the platform edge for nighttime GLOW MODE

### The Tentacles
Each tentacle has a personality. They're not uniform pegs — they're organic, chaotic, reaching in different directions.

| Tentacle | Diameter | Height | Angle | Points | Nickname |
|----------|----------|--------|-------|--------|----------|
| 2× Thick Boys | 2" PVC | 14" | Vertical | 10 pts | Easy pickings |
| 2× Leaners | 1.5" PVC | 20" | 15° lean | 25 pts | Tricky angle |
| 2× Whips | 1" PVC | 28" | Vertical | 50 pts | Thin and tall |
| 1× The Spike | ¾" PVC | 36" | Vertical | 100 pts | Nearly impossible |
| 1× The Curler | 1.5" PVC | 24" | Curved (bent with heat gun) | 75 pts | Rings slide down the curve |

### Ring Mechanics
- Rings are ~10" inner diameter — just barely fits over the Thick Boys without much clearance, requires precision for the Whips
- Glow rings are lightweight — they float/flutter slightly in wind, adding variability outdoors
- Rings that miss land on the ocean platform and stay there (slight lip around edges prevents roll-off)
- Rings that land on a tentacle slide down to the base — satisfying visual confirmation of a hit

### Scoring
- **Standard:** Add up points from all captured tentacles
- **KRAKEN CAPTURED:** 3 rings on the same tentacle = 500 bonus points (stackable — 3 on one + 3 on another = 1000 bonus)
- **CLEAN SWEEP:** At least 1 ring on every tentacle = 300 bonus
- **THE IMPOSSIBLE:** Ring on The Spike = everyone in line gasps

### What Makes It Special

1. **INSTANT RECOGNITION** — Every kid knows ring toss. Zero explanation needed. But the tentacle theme transforms it from "throw rings at pegs" to "CAPTURE THE KRAKEN." The narrative elevates a simple mechanic into an adventure.

2. **GLOW MODE** — UV blacklight + glow rings + bioluminescent tentacles = this game TRANSFORMS at dusk. The ocean platform becomes a glowing deep-sea scene. Rings arc through the air trailing light. This is the carnival's nighttime star.

3. **VARIABLE DIFFICULTY** — 8 tentacles at different heights, angles, and thicknesses means every player finds their level. Kindergartners can hit the Thick Boys from 4ft. 5th graders go for The Spike from 6ft. Adults try for Clean Sweep. Same game, infinite difficulty scaling.

4. **PURE SKILL** — No luck, no randomness, no plinko cascade. You throw the ring, it either lands or it doesn't. Repeated play develops real technique — wrist flick, release angle, spin. Kids who practice between rounds visibly improve. That skill progression is addictive.

5. **THE SPIKE** — Every game needs a white whale. The Spike (¾" PVC, 36" tall, 100 points) is the thing kids talk about. "Did you see Maya hit The Spike?!" becomes carnival legend. It's technically possible but brutally hard. One success makes a hero.

6. **DEAD SIMPLE BUILD** — Plywood base, PVC pipes, paint, glow rings. No moving parts, no acrylic panels, no pneumatics, no electronics. Two dads could build this in an AFTERNOON, not a weekend. Total cost under $80.

7. **PORTABLE** — The whole thing is a 4×4 plywood sheet with PVC pipes that unscrew from floor flanges. Lays flat in a truck bed. Set up in 10 minutes.

## Vibe
Think: deep-sea monster movie meets carnival midway meets rave lighting. The ocean platform is a work of art — painted waves, lurking shadows, tentacles erupting from the surface. Under blacklight it's a bioluminescent nightmare. The glow rings arcing through the air are beautiful. And when one slides down a tentacle to the base, there's that perfect *clink* of success. This game is elegant, visually stunning, and has infinite replay value because you're always trying to hit the next harder tentacle.
