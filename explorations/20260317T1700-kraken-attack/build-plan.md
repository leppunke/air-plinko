# Kraken Attack — Build Plan

## Concept
Giant ring toss carnival game themed as a deep-sea kraken battle. Players throw glow-in-the-dark rings at 8 PVC tentacles of varying heights, thicknesses, and angles rising from a painted ocean platform. Higher/thinner tentacles = more points. Three rings on one tentacle = KRAKEN CAPTURED jackpot.

## Modes
- **Solo (default):** 12 rings, rack up the highest score
- **Head-to-Head:** Two players alternate throws (6 each), or simultaneous from opposite sides with different color rings
- **KRAKEN HUNT:** Cooperative — combine rings to capture as many tentacles as possible (3 rings each = captured)

## Architecture

### 1. Ocean Platform (Base)

The platform is a 4ft × 4ft plywood surface raised on legs, painted as a top-down ocean scene.

- **Surface:** 48"×48" sheet of ¾" plywood
- **Legs:** Four 12" sections of 2×4 lumber, one at each corner, attached with lag bolts
- **Cross braces:** Two 2×4 rails running the 48" width at front and back, connecting the legs
- **Edge lip:** ¾" × 1.5" wood strips around all 4 edges — prevents rings from rolling off the platform
- **Surface paint:** Base coat deep ocean blue/black. Hand-painted or stenciled waves, seaweed, fish, bubbles, coral. The ocean scene is cosmetic but transformative — turns plywood into an undersea world
- **Tentacle mount holes:** 8 holes drilled through the surface at planned positions, fitted with PVC floor flanges (threaded)
- **Anti-tip:** Platform is heavy enough (¾" plywood + legs) to be stable. Optional: sandbags on cross braces underneath

### 2. Tentacles (8 Total)

Each tentacle is a PVC pipe screwed into a floor flange on the platform. Different diameters, heights, and angles create the difficulty spectrum.

| # | Name | PVC Dia. | Height | Angle | Points | Color | Notes |
|---|------|----------|--------|-------|--------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Thick Boy A | 2" | 14" | Vertical (0°) | 10 | Deep purple | Easy target, wide |
| 2 | Thick Boy B | 2" | 14" | Vertical (0°) | 10 | Deep purple | Easy target, wide |
| 3 | Leaner A | 1.5" | 20" | 15° lean left | 25 | Magenta | Ring slides down the lean |
| 4 | Leaner B | 1.5" | 20" | 15° lean right | 25 | Magenta | Mirror of Leaner A |
| 5 | Whip A | 1" | 28" | Vertical (0°) | 50 | Electric green | Thin and tall |
| 6 | Whip B | 1" | 28" | Vertical (0°) | 50 | Electric green | Thin and tall |
| 7 | The Curler | 1.5" | 24" | Curved (~30° arc) | 75 | Hot pink | Bent with heat gun, ring slides down curve |
| 8 | The Spike | ¾" | 36" | Vertical (0°) | 100 | Bioluminescent blue | Nearly impossible, the white whale |

- **Mounting:** Each tentacle screws into a PVC floor flange bolted to the platform surface
- **Lean angles:** Achieved by using 15° PVC elbow fittings at the base, or angled flange mounting with shims
- **The Curler:** Heat-bent 1.5" PVC — use a heat gun to create a smooth ~30° arc in the upper half. Test that a ring can slide down the curve without getting stuck
- **Tentacle caps:** 3D-printed or craft-foam "sucker" tips on each tentacle top — prevents rings from flying off the top, adds visual flair
- **Wrapping:** Pool noodle segments (cut lengthwise, wrapped around) on the lower 4" of each tentacle — adds visual bulk and the "tentacle" organic look. Painted to match tentacle color
- **Paint:** Spray paint each PVC pipe in its assigned color. Optional: UV-reactive spray paint for GLOW MODE

### 3. Tentacle Layout (Top-Down)

Tentacles are arranged organically — NOT in a grid. They should feel like tentacles erupting from the ocean floor.

```
        (looking down at 48" × 48" platform)

     ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
     │                                         │
     │      [Whip B]          [Leaner A]       │
     │        50pt             25pt  /         │
     │                              /          │
     │   [Thick A]    ★SPIKE★    [Curler]      │
     │    10pt        100pt      75pt  )       │
     │                                  )      │
     │        [Leaner B]    [Whip A]           │
     │          25pt \       50pt              │
     │                \                        │
     │            [Thick B]                    │
     │             10pt                        │
     │                                         │
     │              FRONT (throwing line)       │
     └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **The Spike** is center-back — maximum distance from thrower, minimum diameter
- **Thick Boys** are front-center and mid-left — closest to thrower, widest targets
- **Whips and Leaners** fill the middle zone
- **The Curler** is right-center — its curve makes it visually distinctive and mechanically unique

### 4. Glow Rings (Game Pieces)

- **Material option A (budget):** Glow stick necklaces (22") formed into loops and zip-tied. ~$0.25 each. Glow for 4-6 hours. Disposable — buy in bulk (100-pack = $15)
- **Material option B (reusable):** 3D-printed PLA rings (10" inner diameter, ¼" cross-section) spray-painted with UV-reactive paint. Cost: ~$1.50 filament each. Reusable forever under blacklight
- **Material option C (hybrid):** Foam pool noodle rings — cut 10" segments of pool noodle, bend into circle, duct-tape ends together. Spray with glow paint. Cost: ~$0.50 each. Durable, lightweight, reusable
- **Ring count:** 12 per player. Keep 36+ on hand for rapid turnover
- **Ring inner diameter:** ~10" — comfortably fits over the 2" Thick Boys, requires precision for the ¾" Spike
- **Ring cross-section:** ¼"–½" thick — thin enough to slide down tentacles, thick enough to grab

### 5. Scoring Display

- **Points painted on platform:** Each tentacle has its point value painted on the ocean surface around its base
- **Bonus rules sign:** A 24"×18" painted plywood sign mounted on a post next to the throwing line:
  - Point values for each tentacle type
  - KRAKEN CAPTURED: 3 rings on same tentacle = 500 bonus
  - CLEAN SWEEP: 1+ ring on every tentacle = 300 bonus
- **Score tracking:** Operator counts verbally, or a simple whiteboard/chalkboard tallies each throw

### 6. Optional: GLOW MODE (Nighttime)

- **UV blacklight strip:** 4ft LED UV strip mounted along the front edge lip of the platform, angled upward at the tentacles
- **UV-reactive paint:** All tentacles sprayed with UV-reactive overcoat
- **Glow rings:** Use glow stick necklace rings (self-illuminating) OR UV-reactive 3D-printed rings
- **Platform accent:** UV-reactive paint for the fish/waves/bubbles on the ocean surface
- **Effect:** Under blacklight, the tentacles glow alien bioluminescent colors, the ocean surface comes alive, and the glow rings arc through the air trailing light. This is the carnival's nighttime centerpiece

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Platform (Saturday Morning, ~3 hours)
1. Cut plywood to 48"×48" (or buy pre-cut)
2. Cut four 12" sections of 2×4 for legs
3. Cut two 48" sections of 2×4 for cross braces
4. Attach legs to platform corners with lag bolts
5. Attach cross braces between front legs and back legs
6. Cut and attach ¾"×1.5" wood strips around all four edges (edge lip)
7. Drill 8 tentacle mount holes at planned positions (see layout)
8. Bolt PVC floor flanges over each hole from the top
9. Sand everything smooth — no splinters

### Phase 2: Paint the Ocean (Saturday Afternoon, ~2 hours)
1. Base coat entire platform surface ocean blue/black (2 coats)
2. While drying, spray paint all PVC tentacle pipes in assigned colors (2 coats each)
3. Once base coat is dry, paint ocean scene details: waves, fish, seaweed, bubbles, coral. Go wild — this is the art phase
4. Paint point values around each flange mount (10, 25, 50, 75, 100)
5. Seal platform surface with clear polyurethane (protects paint from ring impacts)

### Phase 3: Tentacles (Sunday Morning, ~1 hour)
1. Cut pool noodles into 4" segments, slice lengthwise
2. Wrap pool noodle segments around the base of each tentacle for the organic look
3. Hot-glue or zip-tie the wrapping in place
4. For The Curler: heat-bend the upper half of the 1.5" PVC with a heat gun until it has a smooth ~30° arc. Test with a ring to ensure smooth sliding
5. For Leaners: attach 15° elbow fittings at the base
6. Add 3D-printed sucker caps on tentacle tips (or use PVC end caps painted as suckers)
7. Screw all tentacles into their floor flanges
8. Paint any touch-ups where hardware is visible

### Phase 4: Game Pieces & Finishing (Sunday, ~1 hour)
1. Make glow rings: bend glow stick necklaces into loops, zip-tie. OR: 3D print rings + paint
2. Build scoring sign (paint on scrap plywood or poster board)
3. Set up throwing line (floor tape at 72" from platform front)
4. Optional: mount UV LED strip along front edge lip, wire to battery pack or extension cord
5. Test throws from regulation distance — verify ring sizes work on all tentacles
6. Adjust tentacle positions if any are too easy or too hard to reach

### Phase 5: Testing
1. Test ring toss from 6ft — can you land a ring on The Spike? (should be very hard but possible)
2. Test from 4ft (kid distance) — Thick Boys should be landable ~50% of the time
3. Verify rings slide down all tentacles (especially The Curler) without getting stuck
4. Verify edge lip keeps missed rings on the platform
5. If using GLOW MODE: test at dusk. Verify UV strip illuminates all tentacles. Adjust angle as needed
6. Play a full round. Adjust anything that feels off.

## Safety Notes
- All PVC edges sanded smooth — no sharp cuts
- Platform is heavy and low (12" legs) — very stable, no tip risk
- Rings are lightweight (glow sticks or foam) — zero impact hazard
- No moving parts, no projectiles, no pneumatics
- Edge lip prevents rings from rolling off and becoming a trip hazard
- Throwing line keeps players at safe distance from the platform

## Open Questions
- [ ] Should tentacles be removable (threaded flanges) or permanent (glued)?
  → Recommendation: **Removable.** Thread-in flanges for transport. All tentacles unscrew and lay flat for storage
- [ ] Indoor vs outdoor? Wind affects lightweight glow rings
  → If outdoor, use heavier foam rings (pool noodle material) instead of glow stick necklaces
- [ ] Prize structure — ring count thresholds? (3+ rings landed = small prize, 6+ = medium, Spike = automatic prize)
- [ ] Do we want a tentacle that MOVES? (spring-loaded base, wobbles when hit by a near-miss) — adds chaos but adds complexity
