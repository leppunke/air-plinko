# Buzz Ball — Build Plan

## Concept
Giant honeycomb-themed plinko board with spring plunger launchers, hex peg field, 3 spring-loaded "Bee Bumper" pucks driven by a hand crank, and hexagonal honey pot scoring buckets. Two-player, Grattan honeybee themed.

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Width | 48" (4 ft) | Two players side-by-side |
| Height | 72" (6 ft) | Tall board — big spectacle, visible from far |
| Depth (gap) | 5" | Whiffle ball (2.75") + bumper clearance |
| Tilt angle | ~20° from vertical | Balls stay in play, gravity returns them |
| Peak height (on frame) | ~80" (6'8") | Top of board when tilted on A-frame |

Total footprint with legs: ~48"W × 40"D × 80"H

## Architecture

### 1. Frame & Back Panel
- **Back panel:** 4×6 ft sheet of ¾" plywood
- **Paint:** Matte black base coat, then gold honeycomb pattern stenciled across the entire surface (hexagons ~4" across). Grattan bee logo painted large at top center
- **Edge rails:** 1×6 lumber on all four sides, painted black. Sandwiched between plywood and acrylic
- **"BUZZ BALL" header:** Large cut letters or painted banner across the top rail, gold on black

### 1a. Support Frame (Modular)
- **Rear support legs (×2):** 2×4 lumber, hinged at top, fold flat for storage
- **Cross brace:** 2×4 horizontal between legs at 30" height
- **Angle limiter:** Adjustable chain sets ~20° tilt
- **Base feet:** Rubber-capped 2×4 for grip on grass/concrete
- **Handles (×2):** Heavy-duty gate handles on sides for two-person carry
- **Fasteners:** Wing nuts + carriage bolts throughout — no tools for setup/teardown
- **Labels:** All joints labeled (A1, A2, etc.) for foolproof reassembly

### 2. Front Panel
- **Material:** ¼" clear acrylic sheet, 4×6 ft
- **Mounting:** Bolted through to back panel at edges and through peg points
- **Hex border decals (optional):** Gold vinyl honeycomb border around the edge for extra hive aesthetic

### 3. Honeycomb Peg Field
- **Peg layout:** Hexagonal grid pattern (not square). Pegs offset so each peg has 6 neighbors, creating true honeycomb geometry
- **Peg spacing:** ~5" center-to-center (fits whiffle ball between pegs with clearance)
- **Peg construction:**
  - Base: ¾" wooden dowels, 3.5" long, drilled into plywood
  - Caps: 3D-printed hexagonal caps (1.5" across flats, friction-fit onto dowels). Printed in alternating gold and black
- **Peg count:** ~90 pegs across the 4×6 ft field
- **Clearance zones:** No pegs in the 3 bumper zones (6" diameter clear areas) or the top 8" (launch entry zone)

### 4. Bee Bumpers (×3)
- **What they are:** Large hexagonal pucks (5" across flats, 1.5" thick) painted in yellow/black stripes. When activated, they pop out ~2" from the back panel face, smacking any ball nearby
- **Mechanism:**
  - Each bumper is mounted on a spring-loaded steel rod (½" diameter, 6" long) that passes through a bushing in the back panel
  - A compression spring (behind the panel) keeps the bumper retracted flush with the back panel surface
  - A rotating cam on a shared axle pushes each rod forward against the spring. As the cam rotates past, the spring snaps the bumper back
- **Cam drive:**
  - Single horizontal axle (½" steel rod) runs across the back of the panel at bumper height
  - 3 cams (one per bumper) offset at 120° so they fire in sequence, not simultaneously
  - Axle extends out the right side of the frame to a hand crank with a comfortable grip
  - One full crank rotation = all 3 bumpers fire once each in sequence
- **Positions:** Triangular arrangement in the mid-field:
  - Bumper 1: Left-center, ~30" from bottom
  - Bumper 2: Right-center, ~30" from bottom
  - Bumper 3: Center, ~42" from bottom (higher, near the high-value launch zone)
- **Bumper face:** 3D-printed hex shape with a slightly convex face for maximum ball-scattering angle. Painted in yellow/black concentric rings (like a bee's body)
- **Safety:** Bumpers only extend 2" — well within the 5" gap. Cannot contact acrylic front panel

### 5. Launcher System (×2)
- **Type:** Spring-loaded pinball plunger (same as OG Air Plinko)
- **Position:** Bottom-left and bottom-right corners of the playing field
- **Launch channel:** 1.5" PVC pipe, angled ~75° from horizontal, directs ball upward into the hex field
- **Ball magazine:** Gravity-fed hopper above each launch tube. Holds 5 balls. After each launch, next ball drops into the chamber automatically
- **Pull strength → launch height:**
  - Gentle pull: ball enters field at ~18" (bottom third — short hex run)
  - Full pull: ball enters at ~48" (top zone — long chaotic run through pegs + bumper zone)
- **Parts:**
  - Pinball plunger assemblies (×2, ~$8 each)
  - 1.5" PVC pipe + elbows for launch channel
  - Small plywood hopper box for ball magazine

### 6. Honey Pot Scoring Buckets (×7)
- **Shape:** Hexagonal buckets (3D-printed or plywood-constructed hex boxes)
- **Arrangement:** 7 buckets in a row across the bottom of the board, snug together
- **Collection:** All buckets have holes in the back. Balls roll through to a collection chute behind the board, feeding back to the magazines via gravity
- **Layout (left to right):**

```
 [POLLEN] [HONEY] [ROYAL] [QUEEN] [ROYAL] [HONEY] [POLLEN]
   50       100     200     500     200      100      50
   6"w      5"w     4"w     3"w     4"w      5"w     6"w
```

- **Visual treatment:**
  - QUEEN'S CELL: Smallest, deep gold with a painted crown. Brass bell mounted above it
  - ROYAL JELLY: Purple-gold gradient
  - HONEY: Classic amber gold
  - POLLEN: Yellow-green
- **Brass bell:** Mounted on a bracket above the Queen's Cell. When a ball drops in, the booth volunteer rings it manually (or a simple lever mechanism rings it automatically when the ball hits the bucket floor)

### 7. Ball Return System
- **Behind-the-board chute:** Balls exit scoring buckets through rear holes, drop into an angled collection trough behind the back panel
- **Gravity return:** Trough angles down to left and right, feeding balls back into respective player magazines
- **Center divider:** Splits return flow to correct player side

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Frame & Board (Weekend 1, Day 1)
1. Cut plywood to 48×72"
2. Sand and apply matte black base coat (2 coats, let dry overnight)
3. Cut edge rails from 1×6, attach to plywood edges
4. Build A-frame legs with hinges, cross brace, chain angle limiter
5. Mount handles, rubber feet
6. Label all joints

### Phase 2: Honeycomb Field (Weekend 1, Day 2)
1. Lay out hex grid on plywood: mark peg positions at 5" spacing in hex pattern
2. Mark 3 bumper clearance zones (6" diameter circles — no pegs)
3. Mark launch entry zone at top (no pegs in top 8")
4. Drill ¾" peg holes (~90 holes)
5. Insert and glue dowels (3.5" protruding)
6. 3D print hex peg caps (batch print overnight if needed)
7. Friction-fit hex caps onto all dowels — alternating gold and black

### Phase 3: Bee Bumpers (Weekend 2, Day 1)
1. Cut 3 holes in back panel at bumper positions (½" for pushrods)
2. Install bushings in each hole
3. Mount compression springs on pushrods behind the panel
4. Mount cam axle across the back using pillow block bearings
5. Attach 3 cam lobes at 120° offset
6. Attach hand crank to axle end (extends out right side of frame)
7. 3D print bumper hex pucks, paint yellow/black stripes
8. Attach pucks to pushrod ends (front of board)
9. Test: crank should cause bumpers to pop out ~2" in sequence, then spring back

### Phase 4: Launchers, Buckets, & Ball Return (Weekend 2, Day 2)
1. Build launch channels from PVC pipe at bottom corners, angled ~75°
2. Install pinball plungers at base of launch channels
3. Build ball magazine hoppers from plywood, mount above launch channels
4. Construct 7 scoring buckets (hex shape — plywood with 30° miters or 3D-printed inserts)
5. Mount buckets across bottom of playing field, snug together
6. Drill ball exit holes in bucket backs
7. Build rear collection trough with center divider
8. Route return channels to feed player magazines
9. Test ball flow: launch → hex field → bucket → return → magazine

### Phase 5: Paint, Decorate, & Seal (Weekend 3 or evening sessions)
1. Apply gold honeycomb stencil pattern across back panel (visible through hex field)
2. Paint "BUZZ BALL" header in large letters across top
3. Paint Grattan bee mascot logo at top center
4. Paint scoring buckets in their colors (gold, purple-gold, yellow-green)
5. Apply point value labels to each bucket
6. Mount brass bell above Queen's Cell bucket
7. Drill acrylic panel to match peg holes + edge bolt points
8. Mount acrylic front panel
9. Apply gold honeycomb vinyl border around acrylic edges
10. Seal all edges — no ball escape routes
11. Final playtesting with real kids if possible
