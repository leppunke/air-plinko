# BUZZ BALL

## Concept
A giant honeycomb-themed plinko board where kids LAUNCH balls upward using spring-loaded plungers, then watch them cascade down through a field of hexagonal pegs into honey pot scoring buckets. The twist: three spring-loaded **BEE BUMPERS** — big yellow-and-black hexagonal pucks — randomly POP out from the board face on a crank timer, sending nearby balls ricocheting in wild new directions. A volunteer behind the board cranks a handle that drives the bumper mechanism, controlling the chaos level in real time.

The whole board is a giant honeycomb in Grattan gold and black. The mascot honeybee is everywhere. It looks like you're playing inside a beehive.

## The Pitch
*"Welcome to THE HIVE, Grattan bees! Launch your pollen balls into the honeycomb and watch them BUZZ their way to the honey pots! But watch out — the BEE BUMPERS are coming! Ready? LAUNCH!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×6 ft board tilted at ~20° from vertical, mounted on a sturdy A-frame
- Honeycomb peg field: hexagonal wooden pegs arranged in a hex grid pattern (not square grid — this creates the honeycomb visual AND more chaotic ball paths)
- Two spring-loaded plunger stations at the bottom (one per player)
- Clear acrylic front so everyone watches the chaos
- 5 yellow whiffle balls per player ("pollen balls"), gravity-fed from a magazine

### Mechanics
- **SPRING PLUNGERS:** Pull back, release, ball rockets up a launch tube into the honeycomb field. Pull harder = higher launch = more pegs to bounce off = more chaos = more risk/reward
- **HONEYCOMB PEGS:** Hexagonal wooden pegs (3D-printed hex caps on dowels) arranged in an offset hex grid. Balls bounce between them in gloriously unpredictable hex-angle ricochets
- **BEE BUMPERS (×3):** Three large hexagonal pucks (5" across, painted yellow/black stripes) mounted on spring-loaded rods behind the back panel. Connected via cam mechanism to a hand crank on the side. When the volunteer cranks, bumpers pop out ~2" from the board face in sequence, LAUNCHING any ball that happens to be nearby. Think pinball bumpers but powered by a human
- **HONEY POT BUCKETS:** 7 hexagonal buckets at the bottom, each a different depth and point value. Shaped like honeycomb cells, painted gold. Center "QUEEN'S CELL" is the smallest and deepest

### Scoring
| Bucket | Points | Width | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| QUEEN'S CELL | 500 | 3" | Dead center, tiny hex opening, gold crown painted on it |
| ROYAL JELLY (×2) | 200 | 4" | Flanking the queen, deep purple-gold |
| HONEY (×2) | 100 | 5" | Mid-range, classic gold |
| POLLEN (×2) | 50 | 6" | Outer buckets, wide and easy |

### Win Condition
- **10 balls total** (5 per player), alternating launches
- Highest score wins
- **SWARM BONUS:** If any single ball hits 2+ bee bumpers on the way down before scoring, that ball's points are DOUBLED (the ball "got swarmed")
- **QUEEN BEE JACKPOT:** Hit the Queen's Cell and the booth volunteer rings a big brass bell and the crowd goes wild

## What Makes It Special
1. **The Bee Bumpers** — Nothing like them at any other carnival game. A real human is cranking chaos into the board in real time. Kids watch a ball drifting toward a nice 200-pt bucket and then BWANG a bumper sends it flying. The whole crowd gasps. The volunteer becomes a character — kids love them or hate them
2. **Honeycomb geometry** — Hex pegs create fundamentally different bounce physics than round pegs on a square grid. Balls don't just zigzag left-right, they scatter at 60° angles in six directions. Way more chaotic, way more unpredictable
3. **The aesthetic** — A 4×6 ft honeycomb in Grattan gold and black, covered in bee artwork, with BUZZ BALL in big chunky letters at the top. It looks incredible. Every parent photographs it. It IS the carnival
4. **The sound** — Spring plungers THWACK. Bumpers BONK when they pop. Balls clatter through hex pegs. The brass bell RINGS for the Queen's Cell. This game has a soundtrack
5. **The volunteer factor** — One person behind the board cranking bumpers adds a human chaos element. They can crank fast for mayhem or slow for drama. They become the "beekeeper" — give them a hat and veil. Kids line up just to see what the beekeeper does next
6. **Pure spectator sport** — Clear acrylic front, 6ft tall, balls bouncing through a glowing honeycomb, bumpers popping, bell ringing. Visible from across the schoolyard
7. **Grattan pride** — This isn't a generic game. It's THEIR game. The honeybee. The colors. The hive. Years from now: "Remember Buzz Ball?"

## Vibe
Think: Pinball meets Plinko meets the inside of a beehive, powered by springs and a hand crank and pure elementary school energy. The beekeeper cranks chaos. The crowd roars. A ball hits the Queen's Cell and the bell rings across the yard. A kid walks away buzzing. Literally.
