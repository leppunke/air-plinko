# THUNDER DROPS

## Concept
A storm-chaser themed plinko board designed to assault every single sense a kid has. Players crank a spring-loaded catapult to hurl glowing balls upward into a fog-shrouded, blacklit plinko field where they cascade down through wind chimes, LED lightning, mist jets, and cedar pegs — landing in candy-dispensing scoring buckets at the bottom. Every ball dropped is a full-body weather event.

This isn't a game you play. It's a game that happens TO you.

## The Pitch
*"A STORM IS COMING! Crank your launcher, aim for the eye of the storm, and pray your ball survives the drop! Every bucket drops candy — but only the THUNDER ZONE drops the FULL SIZE BAR. Ready? THREE... TWO... ONE... LAUNCH!"*

## The Five Senses

### 👁️ SIGHT — The Storm Wall
- **Blacklight canopy** over the top of the board. UV-reactive whiffle balls glow electric blue and green as they enter the field
- **LED lightning strips** embedded behind the acrylic — triggered by piezo vibration sensors on key pegs. When a ball hits a wired peg, the whole board FLASHES
- **Fog machine** mounted at the top, blows a slow rolling cloud across the upper third of the board. Balls disappear into fog, then emerge cascading through the pegs below
- **Glow-paint pegs** — alternating UV-reactive neon pink, green, and yellow mushroom-cap pegs. Under blacklight the whole field is a neon forest
- **Scoring buckets light up** when a ball lands — the jackpot bucket strobes gold

### 👂 SOUND — The Soundtrack
- **Wind chime pegs** — every 3rd row of pegs has small aluminum tubes or bells zip-tied to the dowels. As balls cascade through, the board literally plays a random melody. 20 balls in play = a waterfall of chimes
- **Thunder rumble** — a cheap car subwoofer mounted behind the back panel. On jackpot hits, it plays a 2-second thunder crack that you feel in your ribs
- **Rain ambiance** — a small bluetooth speaker plays a looping thunderstorm track (rain + distant thunder) as background atmosphere
- **Hit sounds** — piezo sensors on the scoring buckets trigger different tones: low bucket = dull thud, mid bucket = ding, jackpot = massive thunder crack + crowd cheer
- **Dry fire / miss** — balls that fall through the gutter trigger a sad rain drizzle sound

### ✋ TOUCH — The Storm Hits You
- **Crank catapult launcher** — players grab a textured rubber handle and crank a spring-loaded arm back, then RELEASE. The mechanical snap-back is visceral — way more satisfying than a plunger pull. Full arm engagement, shoulder to fingertips
- **Mist jets** — two garden mister nozzles pointed at the player stations. When a ball enters a scoring bucket, a solenoid valve opens for 0.5 seconds and hits the player with a fine spray. Jackpot = 2-second blast. Kids get WET. On a hot day? They'll line up twice
- **Vibration feedback** — the subwoofer's bass is felt through the frame. Players standing close feel the thunder in their feet and hands
- **Textured balls** — standard whiffle balls plus a few "storm balls" with bumpy 3D-printed shells that bounce unpredictably through the pegs. Chaos multiplier
- **Wind blast** — the fog machine's fan creates a gentle breeze at the top of the board. Players standing close feel the "storm wind" on their face

### 👃 SMELL — Petrichor Station
- **Cedar wood pegs** — real cedar dowels instead of pine. Under the blacklight heat they release a natural warm woodsy smell that fills the booth
- **Essential oil diffuser** — a small ultrasonic diffuser near the fog machine adds petrichor/rain scent to the mist cloud. The whole booth smells like an incoming thunderstorm
- **Mist spray carries scent** — a few drops of eucalyptus or pine oil in the mister water reservoir. When kids get misted, they smell the forest after rain
- Subtle but powerful — the smell is the thing that triggers the memory years later

### 👅 TASTE — The Candy Storm
- **Every scoring bucket is a candy dispenser.** When a ball lands, a gravity-fed tube behind the board drops candy into a collection tray at the player's station
- **Candy tiers:**
  - Gutter (0 pts): No candy. Sad trombone.
  - Outer buckets (50 pts): Single Jolly Rancher
  - Mid buckets (100 pts): Fun-size candy bar
  - Inner buckets (200 pts): Full gummy pack or Airheads bundle
  - **THUNDER ZONE jackpot (500 pts):** FULL SIZE CANDY BAR drops from a golden chute with maximum fanfare
- **Rock candy "lightning bolts"** — available at the booth as bonus prizes for high scores. They look like actual lightning
- Kids walk away literally tasting the storm

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×6 ft plinko board, tilted ~20° from vertical, mounted at ~7ft peak height
- Blacklight canopy across the top, fog machine behind the crown
- Two crank-catapult stations on the ground, one per player
- 10 UV-reactive whiffle balls per player, gravity-fed magazine
- Mist jets aimed at player stations
- Candy tubes loaded behind the board

### Mechanics
- **CRANK LAUNCHER:** A geared crank arm (like a medieval catapult meets a jack-in-the-box). Player cranks the handle 3-5 turns to tension the spring, places the ball in the cup, then pulls the release lever. THWACK — ball launches upward into the fog zone at the top of the board
- **VARIABLE POWER:** More cranks = more tension = higher launch. But over-crank and the ball flies over the board entirely (a "lightning strike" — dramatic but zero points)
- **PEG FIELD:** Classic plinko offset rows. Every 3rd peg has a wind chime. Every 5th peg has a piezo LED trigger. Cedar wood throughout
- **SCORING BUCKETS:** 7 buckets across the bottom, color-coded by point value, each connected to a candy dispensing tube
- **THE FOG ZONE:** Top third of the board is shrouded in fog. Balls enter here first — the crowd can't see where the ball goes until it drops below the cloud line. Anticipation is ELECTRIC
- **60-second rounds.** Go.

### Win Condition
- **Competitive:** Highest total score after 10 balls each
- **Speed bonus:** +100 pts for launching all 10 before time expires
- **Lightning Strike penalty:** Over-cranked balls that fly over = -50 pts (but the crowd goes "OHHH")

## What Makes It Special
1. **FIVE SENSES, ONE GAME.** No carnival game hits smell and taste. This one does. The fog, the cedar, the mist, the candy, the chimes — it's a complete sensory environment, not just a game
2. **The Fog Zone** — balls vanish into clouds, then emerge cascading through neon pegs. The 2 seconds of not knowing where your ball went creates genuine suspense for players AND spectators
3. **Candy rain** — every ball that scores drops candy. Kids are playing for IMMEDIATE sugar rewards, not tickets. The dopamine loop is instantaneous
4. **The crank** — winding up the catapult builds tension and anticipation. The release is explosive. Way more dramatic than a button or stomp
5. **Weather immersion** — mist, fog, wind, thunder bass, rain sounds, petrichor smell — the booth IS a thunderstorm. Kids step into a different world for 60 seconds
6. **Gutter punishment** — miss the buckets and you get misted in the face with zero candy. The crowd laughs. The kid laughs. Everyone has a good time
7. **Night mode** — this game gets BETTER after dark. Blacklights, LED lightning, glowing fog, neon pegs — it transforms into a rave plinko
8. **Spectator magnet** — the fog, the lights, the chime cascade, the thunder rumble, the SCREAMING — you hear and see this booth from 100 feet away
