# STOMP ROCKETS PLINKO

## Concept
A full-body carnival game where kids stomp on air bladders to launch whiffle balls UPWARD into a giant tilted plinko board mounted above them. Balls rocket up through launch tubes, burst out at different heights on the board, then cascade down through a forest of neon-colored pegs into scoring buckets. The harder and faster you stomp, the higher the ball goes — and the higher-value scoring zones it can reach on the way back down.

Two players. Two stomp pads. One giant board. Pure stomping pandemonium.

## The Pitch
*"STOMP as hard as you can! Launch balls into the sky and watch them bounce their way to MEGA POINTS! Ready? THREE... TWO... ONE... STOMP!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant 4×6 ft plinko board tilted at ~25° from vertical, mounted on a frame at ~7ft peak height
- Two stomp pad stations on the ground in front of the board, one per player
- Each stomp pad connects to a launch tube via flexible air hose
- 10 whiffle balls per player, loaded into a gravity-fed magazine above each stomp pad
- Clear acrylic front panel so spectators can watch the ball chaos from behind

### Mechanics
- **STOMP PADS:** Heavy-duty air bladders on the ground (think: oversized whoopee cushion meets stomp rocket). Each stomp compresses the bladder, shooting a burst of air through a hose into the launch tube
- **LAUNCH TUBES:** Angled PVC tubes mounted at the bottom of the board. Air burst from the stomp pad shoots one ball up the tube and into the playing field. Ball auto-feeds from the magazine
- **BALL MAGAZINE:** Gravity-fed hopper above the launch tube. After each stomp, the next ball drops into the launch chamber automatically. No reloading — just STOMP STOMP STOMP
- **PEG FIELD:** Classic plinko peg layout with offset rows, but the pegs are chunky 3D-printed mushroom-cap shapes in neon colors (pink, green, yellow, blue). Balls bounce chaotically between them
- **SCORING BUCKETS:** 7 buckets across the bottom of the board, each a different point value. Center bucket is narrow and worth 500 pts. Outer buckets are wider, worth 50 pts.
- **HEIGHT ZONES:** The launch tube deposits balls at different heights depending on stomp force:
  - WEAK stomp → ball enters at the bottom third (shorter peg run, lands in wider low-value buckets)
  - MEDIUM stomp → ball enters mid-board (decent peg run, can reach mid-value buckets)
  - MONSTER stomp → ball reaches the top zone (longest peg run, most chaos, chance at the 500-pt center bucket or the dreaded GUTTER)
- **THE GUTTER:** Two narrow slots on the far edges of the board. If a ball falls in the gutter, zero points AND a loud sad trombone sound. High risk of gutters from monster stomps — the ball enters at the top where the board is narrowest

### Scoring
| Zone | Points | Width | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|-------|
| CENTER JACKPOT | 500 | 3" | Tiny slot dead center, hardest to hit |
| INNER (×2) | 200 | 4" | Flanking the jackpot |
| MID (×2) | 100 | 5" | Reliable mid-value |
| OUTER (×2) | 50 | 6" | Wide and forgiving |
| GUTTER (×2) | 0 | 2" | The edges of shame + sad trombone |

### Win Condition
- **45-second round.** As many stomps as you can.
- Each stomp launches one ball (if you stomp before the next ball loads, it's a "dry fire" — satisfying whoopee cushion fart sound, no ball)
- **Competitive mode:** Highest total score wins
- **Speed bonus:** +100 pts for launching all 10 balls before time runs out

## What Makes It Special
1. **FULL BODY gameplay** — kids don't just stand there, they're JUMPING and STOMPING. Exhausting and hilarious. The line watches kids going absolutely feral on the stomp pad
2. **The sound** — every stomp makes a deeply satisfying pneumatic THWOMP. Dry fires make a fart sound. Gutters trigger a sad trombone. The 500-pt jackpot triggers a siren. This game has a SOUNDTRACK
3. **Visible chaos** — clear acrylic front means the whole crowd watches 20 balls cascading through neon pegs simultaneously. It's a living lava lamp of bouncing balls
4. **Skill expression** — controlled stomps aim for mid-board consistency. Monster stomps go for the jackpot but risk the gutter. Do you stomp smart or stomp hard? Kids develop strategies after their first round
5. **Self-resetting** — balls fall into buckets, roll through collection tubes behind the board, gravity-feed back to the magazines. The game basically runs itself
6. **The exhaustion factor** — after 45 seconds of maximum stomping, kids walk away panting and grinning. Parents get a 30-second breather
7. **Spectator sport** — the board is huge, visible from 30 feet, and the ball chaos is mesmerizing. Draws a crowd

## Vibe
Think: Dance Dance Revolution meets Plinko meets that pure primal joy of jumping in puddles. Controlled violence against a floor pad, rewarded with beautiful chaos above. Kids will beg to go again.
