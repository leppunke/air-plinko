# SLINGSHOT SIEGE

## Concept
Two players face off across a FORTRESS WALL — a 5×4 ft vertical plinko board with castle gates (pass-through holes) at varying heights. Each player wields a swivel-mounted slingshot, launching whiffle balls through the gates to invade the opponent's side. Balls that breach a gate cascade down through a field of neon pegs on the opponent's half, tumbling into scoring moats at the bottom. Higher gates are harder to hit but unleash longer, wilder cascades worth more points. Lower gates are easy pickings but score peanuts.

This isn't parallel play. This is WAR. You're staring your opponent down through the arrow slits, launching siege ammunition, watching their face when your ball threads the top gate and pinballs through their entire kingdom into the royal moat for 500 points.

## The Pitch
*"You're under siege! Launch your balls through the castle gates and rain chaos on your opponent's kingdom! Aim high for glory or aim low for safety — but watch out, they're shooting back! Ready your slingshots... FIRE!"*

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Giant fortress wall: 5ft wide × 4ft tall plinko board, standing vertical on a sturdy base frame
- The wall has **6 castle gates** (pass-through holes) at three height tiers — 2 per tier
- Each side of the wall has its own peg field and scoring moats
- Two slingshot stations, one on each side, about 5ft back from the wall
- 12 whiffle balls per player (red vs blue)
- Clear acrylic panels on both sides so spectators can watch the dual cascades

### The Wall
The fortress wall is the centerpiece. It's a vertical plinko board with pegs on BOTH sides. Cut through the board at various heights are the castle gates — rectangular openings just big enough for a whiffle ball to pass through. Each gate has a 3D-printed drawbridge frame with angled edges that reject off-center shots, so you need to hit the sweet spot.

### Gate Tiers
| Gate | Height | Opening Width | Cascade Length | Scoring Multiplier |
|------|--------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| MAIN GATE (×2) | 12" from bottom | 6" wide | Short — 3 peg rows | 1× (base bucket value) |
| ARROW SLIT (×2) | 28" from bottom | 4.5" wide | Medium — 6 peg rows | 2× (double bucket value) |
| TOWER GATE (×2) | 42" from bottom | 3.5" wide | Long — 9 peg rows | 3× (triple bucket value) |

### Scoring Moats (each side, mirrored)
| Zone | Base Points | Width | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|-------|
| ROYAL MOAT (center) | 250 | 4" | Narrow center channel, jackpot zone |
| KNIGHT'S MOAT (×2) | 100 | 5" | Flanking the center |
| PEASANT'S MOAT (×2) | 50 | 6" | Outer channels |
| THE SWAMP (×2) | 0 | 3" | Edge gutters — zero points, sad horn |

**Final score = Base moat points × Gate tier multiplier**

So a ball through a TOWER GATE (3×) into the ROYAL MOAT (250) = **750 points**. A ball through a MAIN GATE (1×) into the PEASANT'S MOAT (50) = **50 points**. Risk. Reward. SIEGE.

### The Slingshots
- Heavy-duty Y-frame (3D-printed or bent steel rod) mounted on a swivel bolt on a post
- Surgical tubing (Theraband Gold — the good stuff) provides the launch power
- Leather/canvas pouch sized for a whiffle ball
- Swivel mount limits aim to a ~60° cone aimed at the wall — cannot aim at people
- Pull-back distance: ~8–12" gives enough force to reach the tower gates from 5ft away
- Players load, aim, pull, and release. Reload takes ~3 seconds. Pure slingshot satisfaction.

### Rounds
- **Simultaneous fire.** Both players launch at will — no turns. Load and fire as fast as you can.
- 12 balls each. When you're out, you're out.
- **No time limit** — the round ends when both players have fired all 12 balls.
- Optional: 60-second timer for carnival throughput (unfired balls don't score)
- Highest total score wins

### Ball Return
- Scoring moats angle backward into a collection chute behind each player's side of the wall
- Balls that miss the wall entirely roll into a floor gutter and get collected by the operator
- Balls that hit the wall but don't go through a gate bounce back toward the shooter — they can pick it up and re-fire (incentivizes fast retrieval!)
- Bounce-backs are part of the game — scrambling to grab your ricochets adds frantic energy

## What Makes It Special

1. **FACE-TO-FACE COMBAT** — You're staring down your opponent through the castle gates. Eye contact. Trash talk. A whiffle ball whizzes past their face through a gate and they watch it demolish their peg field. This is PERSONAL.

2. **SLINGSHOTS** — Every kid on earth wants to shoot a slingshot. The pull-back tension, the snap of release, the satisfying arc of the ball — this is primordial fun. No kid has ever shot a slingshot at a plinko board. Until now.

3. **DUAL CASCADES** — Both sides of the wall are active simultaneously. Spectators gather on both sides watching independent plinko chaos. A ball breaches the top gate on Side A — the crowd on Side B watches it cascade through 9 rows of neon pegs. Meanwhile Side A's crowd is tracking an incoming ball through their pegs. It's TWO spectacles in one game.

4. **RISK/REWARD MARKSMANSHIP** — Aim for the tiny tower gate at the top for a 3× multiplier? Or play it safe with the wide main gates at the bottom? A kid who drills 3 tower shots could beat someone who sinks all 12 through the main gate. But missing the tower gate entirely means the ball bounces back and you've wasted time. Accuracy vs. speed vs. risk.

5. **THE RICOCHET SCRAMBLE** — Missed shots bounce back toward you. Quick players grab them and re-fire. Slow players waste time. The floor around the slingshot station becomes a frantic ball-retrieval zone. Kids diving for ricochets while trying to reload is peak carnival chaos.

6. **THE NARRATIVE** — "Siege the castle" is immediately understood by every kid who's ever played with blocks, seen a movie, or had an imagination. The fortress wall, the gates, the moats — this game has a STORY. Kids don't just play it, they roleplay it. "I GOT YOUR ROYAL MOAT!"

7. **SPECTATOR SPORT** — The fortress wall is a 5×4 ft visual landmark. Neon pegs. Balls flying through the air. Dual cascades. Two kids going absolutely feral with slingshots. This draws a crowd from across the carnival.

8. **SELF-REGULATING DIFFICULTY** — Younger kids stand closer (3ft from wall, easier to hit gates). Older kids stand at full distance (5ft, regulation). The slingshot force and aim difficulty scale naturally with the player. No rule changes needed.

## Vibe
Think: Angry Birds in real life meets castle siege meets that scene in every medieval movie where flaming projectiles arc over the walls. Controlled marksmanship rewarded with beautiful chaos. The slingshot pull-back creates a moment of tension — aim, breathe, release — then pure pandemonium as the ball crashes through the peg field. Kids will be talking about "that time I hit the tower gate and got 750 points" for YEARS.
