Perform the following actions to iteratively brainstorm improvements to our carnival game. 

1. Figure out how to capture a screenshot of diagram.html in the current the environment. I believe some headless chrome or playwright version may be available. See what you can set up. Make it happen in whatever way gets the job done. Save any learnings to ./skills/screenshot.md.
2. Read the ./build-plan.md. Imagine a line of elementary schoolers taking turns playing this at a school carnival. Also imagine two dads assembling it over the course of a few weekends. 
3. Create or open the explorations/ folder. Find something interesting to riff on, or just rool with the OG concept.
5. Mix it the fuck up. Play with how it gets built, the colors, the style, the mechanics, bend the gameplay, bend the rules, bend everything but physics itself. We are making core memories out of home depot lumber, 3D printed parts, and a bunch of M18 milwaukee tools looking for work. Kids talking about that crazy rad game for years to come is the goal.
6. Once you have a fun idea, create a unique datetime prefixed folder titled after your concept.
7. Add a README.md describing the concept and gameplay.
8. Add a build-plan.md for the assembly of the updated concept.
9. Generate a diagram.html of a 3D model based on the build plan.
10. Capture a screenshot of diagram.html and save it as diagram.png in that folder.
11. Evaluate how closely diagram.png captures the ethos of the ethos of the concept in the README.md. If it is not an obvious near-perfect representation, modify the build-plan.md to correct for ambiguities. Save any learning on effective descriptions to ./skills/cad-architect.md
12. Once the diagram accurately represents the game. Create an EVAL.md and populate it with the scoring of the game on a set or carnival game heurists:
    - Overall entertainment
    - Memorablity
    - Ease of construction
    - Appeal by elementary school grade
    - Skill to luck spectrum (both can be good, ideally some of both)
    - Chaos level
    - Durability (or can fragile parts be quickly swapped)
    - Photogenicity
    - Onboarding time - (cornhole vs dungeons and dragons)
